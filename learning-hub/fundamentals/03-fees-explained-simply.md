# 03 · Amazon Fees, Explained Simply

*The numbers that decide whether a deal makes money. Verified for 2026.
For the deep, fully-sourced tables, see [`../../01_research_brief.md`](../../01_research_brief.md) §3.*

---

## The fees that hit every sale

1. **Selling-plan fee**
   - Individual: **$0.99 per item** (no monthly fee)
   - Professional: **~$40/month** (no per-item fee) — worth it past ~40 sales/mo, and required for the Buy Box.

2. **Referral fee** — Amazon's commission, **~15%** of the sale price for most
   categories (range 8–15%; a few higher). $0.30 minimum.

3. **FBA fulfillment fee** — per-unit pick/pack/ship, by size & weight. Rough 2026 points:
   | Size tier (example) | ~Fee/unit |
   |---|---|
   | Small standard (≤12 oz) | ~$3.20 |
   | Large standard (2–2.5 lb, common) | ~$6.60 |
   | Large bulky (21–50 lb) | ~$10+ |
   - Plus a **3.5% fuel surcharge** on the fulfillment fee (since April 2026, ~$0.17/unit).

## Fees that hit if you're not careful

- **Storage** — per cubic foot per month; **higher Oct–Dec**. Cheap to ignore at low volume, painful if you overstock.
- **Aged-inventory surcharge** — kicks in at **181 days** sitting in the warehouse. Slow movers get expensive at ~6 months.
- **Low-inventory-level fee** — penalty on fast SKUs you let run too low. Restock around 45–60 days of supply.
- **Inbound placement fee** — when Amazon splits your shipment; largely avoidable.

> **Bottom line:** all-in, Amazon's fees commonly eat **20–40% of the sale price**.

## A real Online-Arbitrage example

You find a product selling for **$30** on Amazon that you can buy for **$14**:

| Line | Amount |
|---|---|
| Sale price | **$30.00** |
| − Referral fee (15%) | −$4.50 |
| − FBA fulfillment (~standard) | −$4.75 |
| − Fuel surcharge (3.5%) | −$0.17 |
| − Cost of goods (COGS) | −$14.00 |
| **= Profit (before prep/returns/ads)** | **≈ $6.58** |
| **ROI** (profit ÷ COGS) | **≈ 47%** |
| **Margin** (profit ÷ sale price) | **≈ 22%** |

OA sellers typically want **≥30% ROI** *after* prep and an allowance for returns,
because some buys won't sell or will come back. This is exactly the math SellerAmp
SAS and Amazon's Revenue Calculator do for you in seconds.

## The one rule that prevents the most expensive mistake

**Model the fees BEFORE you buy — never after.** Every single product, run it
through SellerAmp or the Revenue Calculator first. The #1 beginner money-loss is
buying inventory and *then* discovering the fees erased the margin. Our AI scout
also estimates this automatically, but always confirm a real SKU before committing
cash — actual FBA fees depend on exact dimensions, not just weight.
