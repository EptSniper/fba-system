## Why it matters for this project
FBA reimbursements are a profit lever the corpus hasn't covered yet, and the Nov-2025 policy shift makes
accurate per-unit Cost-of-Goods a hard requirement — which ties directly into the honest-COGS discipline the
bookkeeping sources already push, and is a plausible future control-center signal (reimbursement tracking).

## Key takeaways
1. **[policy] Reimbursement valuation changed (effective Nov 15 2025):** Amazon now reimburses lost/damaged/
   destroyed FBA inventory at **manufacturing / sourcing cost, not retail selling price**. Sellers report
   recoveries down 50–75% (a $40 retail SKU with $9 landed cost now reimburses ~$9, previously ~$40).
2. **Two mandatory defensive actions:** (a) enter accurate **Cost of Goods on every active SKU**
   (Inventory → Manage All Inventory) — a missing/zero cost field defaults the payout to near-zero;
   (b) **submit supplier-invoice cost documentation within 60 days** of a claim, or Amazon uses its own
   (consistently lower) internal estimate.
3. **Nine claimable categories:** lost-in-warehouse (~35–40% of all claims), damaged-in-warehouse,
   lost-in-inbound-shipment, customer-return-not-received (5–10% of claimed returns never arrive),
   removal-shipment lost/damaged, fee overcharges (wrong dimensions/weight → higher fee tier),
   switcheroo returns (customer returns a different/broken unit, restocked as sellable), multi-channel-
   fulfillment errors, duplicate refunds.
4. **Filing hygiene:** claims need SKU / FNSKU / quantity / transaction-ID (from the Inventory
   Reconciliation Report) / date. Vague claims ("some inventory missing") get denied; documented inbound
   claims hit **90%+ approval vs <40% without** photos/packing lists. First-line support denies ~40% of
   valid claims — escalate with evidence.
5. **Windows & scale:** ~18-month claim window for most types, **but removal orders are only 90 days**.
   Industry rule of thumb: 1–3% of FBA inventory has a reimbursable issue per year; ~$1,500–3,000/yr is
   typically recoverable. Third-party recovery services take ~20–25% of recovered funds. Run a monthly
   30–60 min audit rather than 18-month catch-ups.
