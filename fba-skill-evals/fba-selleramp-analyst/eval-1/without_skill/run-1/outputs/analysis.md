## Short answer
**Not at $15. Pass — or get the cost down to $14 or below.**

The single most important line on that SellerAmp panel is **Max Cost = $14.00**. Max Cost is SellerAmp's reverse calculation: the highest price you can pay and still hit your target ROI. Your cost of $15 is **$1.00 ABOVE that ceiling**, which is exactly why your ROI landed at 28% instead of 30%. SellerAmp is telling you "you overpaid by a dollar."

## Why this fails the gate
The operation's buy criteria (single source of truth, ai-brain.json) require:
- **Minimum ROI: 30%** — you are at **28%**. FAIL.
- Minimum profit/unit: $3.00 — you are at $4.20. Pass.
- Price band $8–$60 — $26 sell price. Pass.

ROI is the binding constraint. 28% < 30%, so this is a **PASS** under the project's own rules. It's close, not crazy — but the whole point of the Max Cost field is to stop you buying at exactly this kind of "just barely under" cost.

## The fix: buy at $14, not $15
- At **$14 cost** you hit the SAS Max Cost and clear the 30% ROI gate (~$5.20 profit, ~30%+ ROI).
- Every dollar off the cost flows straight to profit and ROI. Look for a coupon, cashback (Rakuten/TopCashback), a discounted gift card, or a sale to manufacture that extra margin — the operation's standard stacking play.
- If you genuinely cannot get below $14, this is a pass. Don't talk yourself into a 28% deal; that's the trap Max Cost exists to prevent.

## Sanity check on the SAS figures (they're consistent)
- Profit $4.20 = $26 sell − $15 cost − **~$6.80 total fees** (referral + FBA fulfillment). Consistent.
- ROI 28% = $4.20 / $15. Correct.
- Margin = $4.20 / $26 = **16.2%** — thin; little cushion if fees rise or you must drop price.
- Breakeven $19.50 means price can fall ~25% before you lose money — a decent buffer, but breakeven only protects against price drops; it does nothing about the cost being too high.

## What this check does NOT cover
SellerAmp profitability is only half a buy decision. Before buying you still need the **demand and eligibility** read, none of which is in these numbers:
- **BSR / monthly sales** — will it sell? (gate: BSR ≤ 200k, ≥ 50 sales/mo)
- **Offer count** — 3–25 sellers (avoid 1–2 = IP risk; 25+ = race to the bottom)
- **Buy Box / Amazon-on-listing** — hard reject if Amazon holds the Buy Box
- **Keepa history** — is $26 the real price or a spike? Are offers rising?
- **Eligibility / IP risk** — ungated? brand a complaint-filer? hazmat/meltable?

A green-ish SellerAmp panel with weak demand or an IP-risky brand is still a no-buy.

## Bottom line
Good price to sell at, but you're paying **$1 too much**. At $15 it's a 28% ROI deal that fails the 30% gate. **Get the cost to $14 or below and it clears.** No purchase is approved here — that decision stays with you.
