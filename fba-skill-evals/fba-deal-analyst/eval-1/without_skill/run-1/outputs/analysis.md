# Deal Analysis: Amazon in the Buy Box

## The numbers you gave

| Metric | Value | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Sell price | $42 | Fine |
| Landed cost | $19 | Fine |
| ROI (as you framed it) | "50%+" | Gross, not net — see below |
| BSR | ~9,000 | Healthy velocity in most categories |
| Sellers | 6 | Moderate competition |
| Amazon in buy box | ~50% of last 12 months | **This is the dealbreaker** |

## Short answer

**Pass on this one — or at most do a tiny test buy.** Everything looks great except the one thing that matters most for an OA flip: Amazon is on the listing and holds the buy box about half the time. When Amazon is a seller actively winning the buy box, this is normally a "no buy" for resellers, and ~50% presence is well past the threshold where I'd walk.

## Why the Amazon line kills it

The buy box is where roughly 80-90% of sales happen. If Amazon owns it ~50% of the time:

1. **You only sell when Amazon is out of stock or rotates out.** That remaining ~50% gets split among the *other 5 third-party sellers* — so your true share of sales is a fraction of half, not half.
2. **Amazon wins the buy box at price parity.** To take it while they're present, you generally have to undercut Amazon, which torches the margin you're excited about.
3. **Amazon can restock anytime.** Their on/off ~50% pattern means they dip out and come back. You can land units right as they restock and sit on inventory for weeks.
4. **Capital drag.** Money tied up in slow-moving units is worse than a lower-ROI item that actually turns.

## Re-checking your ROI math

"$42 sells, $19 landed, 50%+" is a *gross* spread, not net. You still owe:
- Amazon referral fee (~15% of $42 ≈ $6.30)
- FBA fulfillment fee (~$4-7 depending on size/weight)
- Inbound shipping, prep, returns

Rough net per unit: $42 − $6.30 − ~$5 − $19 ≈ **~$11.70 profit (~62% ROI) — but only if it sells at $42.** With Amazon in the box half the time, you likely won't hold $42; to move units you'll drop into the $36-38 range and realized ROI falls toward 30-40%, on units that turn slowly.

## What would change my answer

Only if **all** of these were true:
- Amazon's buy box presence is clearly **trending down** over the last 90 days.
- The Buy Box price is **stable at ~$42**, not being hammered by the 6 sellers.
- Sales velocity is high enough that even a 1/3 share clears your units in 30-45 days.
- You can land at $19 with room to undercut and still profit.

## Recommended next steps

1. **Open Keepa's Buy Box statistics tab** and get the exact Amazon buy box %. If 90-day Amazon presence ≥ ~30%, hard pass.
2. **Count sales-rank drops** over 90 days to estimate monthly unit sales.
3. **Recompute net profit at $37**, the price you'll likely have to hold.
4. If you still want exposure, **buy 1-3 units max** as a probe.

## Bottom line

The spread looks beautiful, but Amazon at ~50% buy box turns "great on paper" into "slow capital with a margin you'll have to give away." For a clean OA flip, **this is a pass.**
