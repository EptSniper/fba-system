# Amazon Arbitrage: The Complete Guide for 2026 (ScoutClaw)

Standard, well-structured OA/RA primer. Durable points that corroborate the project's brain:

## Fundamentals
- Arbitrage = buy low from a retailer, resell higher on Amazon, profit = spread − fees. First-sale doctrine
  makes it legal in the US; 3P sellers are 60%+ of Amazon units. Gating/category restrictions are Amazon
  policy (not legality) and need invoices to ungate.
- RA vs OA: start with RA to learn (low cost, local exclusivity, doesn't scale); scale with OA (nationwide,
  automatable, higher competition + logistics cost).

## Buy discipline (matches ai-brain gates)
- **6-step workflow:** find discount → verify exact ASIN → calculate net profit → buy → prep+ship → Amazon
  fulfills.
- **Target ≥30% ROI after all fees** ("most experienced sellers"); guide's ScoutClaw tool uses a looser
  15%+ threshold (note the gap — vendor deal-flow bar is lower than a disciplined buyer's bar).
- Net Profit = Sale − referral (≈15%) − FBA fulfilment ($3.22–$6.75+) − COGS − inbound shipping − prep.
- **Start with 3–5 units** of any new ASIN, confirm sell-through, then scale. Buying 50–100 units before
  one sale is the classic capital trap.
- **BSR discipline:** keep BSR under ~100k in most categories; a climbing (worsening) BSR = fading demand.
- **ASIN-match traps:** color/size/model/pack variations are different ASINs with different prices — the
  most common costly error.
- **Returns buffer:** build 5–10% for high-return categories (apparel/shoes 15–25%); "if a deal only works
  at 0% returns, it doesn't work."
- **Brand/IP risk:** research the brand first; avoid known reseller-targeting brands, especially on a young
  account.

## 2026 reality check
- Margins compressed (deals that yielded ~40% now ~25%); FBA/storage/referral fees up; more gating; IP-
  complaint risk. Still viable if treated as a disciplined business (consistent sourcing, careful math,
  reinvest 70–100% early profits, use tools).
- Realistic net margins ~25–35% for disciplined experienced sellers; income scales with sourcing volume
  (profit/unit is roughly constant, deal-count is the lever).
