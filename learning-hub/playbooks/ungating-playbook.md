# Ungating Playbook

*How to get approved to sell gated brands — for free. Distilled from the Ungate
Master Guide (cAKdhzKFIzE) plus the case studies. Ungating is the #1 roadblock for
new sellers; this removes it.*

---

## The 3 types of products
1. **Auto-ungated** — sellable immediately, no invoice. The more you sell, the more Amazon trusts you and the more brands auto-ungate. **Focus here first** to build sales history. (e.g. Stafford, Cuddle Duds, many Walmart items.)
2. **Gated → ungate via invoice** — the most common. Requires **10 / 50 / 100 / 200 units** depending on the brand.
3. **Hard-gated / restricted** — you can't even apply ("Copy listing" only). Only grandfathered sellers can sell. (Nike, Adidas, etc.) **Skip these as a beginner.**

> Always click **"Apply to sell → Request approval"** on anything gated — sometimes it instantly approves (a hidden auto-ungate). You never know until you try.

## Find auto-ungates fast — Boxem bulk checker (new, 2026)
A lot of products are **auto-ungated** for beginners; the win is finding them fast.
- **Boxem** (boxem.com, 2-week free trial) has a **bulk ungate checker** — paste in ASINs (or use its **suggested
  auto-ungate ASIN list**) and it checks **~5 brands/second** what your account can already sell. Do this *while
  storefront-stalking* — paste each seller's ASINs to instantly see what needs no documentation.
- As a beginner, **mostly buy auto-ungates**, and knock out **one or two big gated brands** on the side.
- Milestones (**~2K sales, 10K sales, 6 months in**) auto-unlock a lot more brands.

## How to ungate (step by step)
1. In Seller Central, search the brand → select **New** condition → **Apply to sell → Request approval**.
2. You'll get an upload page (check the boxes, drop a PDF). It may say 200 units, but **10 units is usually accepted**.
3. **Buy ~10 units** of one product of that brand from a **known-good source** (see below) and get the **invoice**.
4. Upload the invoice PDF. Optionally add a **photo of the 10 units laid out** (more info → higher approval odds). Rename the PDF sensibly.
5. If denied, **resubmit**. It often takes **5–10, sometimes 30–50** tries. Bigger brand + newer account = harder.

### What counts as a valid invoice
- The document must say **"Invoice"** (not "Order Confirmation").
- It must show **your Seller Central shipping address** and the **item name/quantity**.
- You do **NOT** need a wholesaler/distributor — buying **direct from the brand's website** or a known-good retailer works.
- **Best beginner-friendly source: `target.com`** — its receipts say "Invoice." (~$80 for 10 units ungated Crayola in the guide.) Crayola/grocery via Target can be ~$0.50–8/unit.
- **Strengthen a submission:** combine the **order-confirmation email + invoice + a photo of the units + the shipping/delivery email** into one PDF, and add a short **ChatGPT-written cover letter**. Brand websites also work (Gap for Gap, Nautica for Nautica, **Frontier Co-op**, **EE Distribution**).

---

## The instant-denial trap (important)
- If your application is **denied in under ~5–10 minutes**, a **bot auto-denied** it — resubmitting the same thing won't work.
- Fix: give it a **24–48h cooldown**, submit on a **different ASIN** of the brand, or **reply to the case** directly.
- You want the application to sit **"under review" for more than 5–10 minutes** (a human is looking).

---

## Money-saving tactics (use sparingly, within policy)
- **Ungate for "free":** buy the 10 units, get ungated, then **return them within the retailer's window** (Target ~30–60 days). Don't abuse it.
- **Don't pay ungating services** — people charge $500+. The DIY process above is free.

---

## Strategy: build trust first
New accounts should **focus on auto-ungated brands and break even** to rack up sales velocity. More sales →
Amazon trusts the account → harder brands ungate more easily later. Don't burn early energy chasing
hard-gated Nike/Adidas profit (a lesson from the Peter & King interviews).
