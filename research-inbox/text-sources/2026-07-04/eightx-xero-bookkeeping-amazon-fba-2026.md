## Distilled content

**Core rule (reinforces staged Eightx/Beancount notes):** never record Amazon's net deposit as revenue. Their example: a $47,000 deposit = $68,000 gross sales − $8,500 referral − $6,200 FBA fulfillment − $1,800 storage − $2,100 advertising − $2,400 refunds. Book gross, break out every fee type. Keep Amazon fees out of COGS (as variable expenses below gross margin) — buried fees also wreck due diligence at exit.

**Tool tiering:** A2X is the gold standard for Amazon→Xero for sellers >$50K/mo ($69/mo Standard; posts each settlement as properly-coded journal entries, penny-perfect reconciliation, multi-region). Link My Books ($9–49/mo) for <$50K/mo. Xero Growing plan $42/mo; realistic all-in $611–2,111/mo incl. bookkeeper; one-time setup $1.5–3.5K.

**Chart of accounts:** separate revenue accounts (FBA sales, FBM sales, shipping income, other income, refunds as contra-revenue); COGS = product landed cost + inbound freight + prep ONLY; separate expense accounts per fee type (referral, FBA fulfillment, storage, advertising, other); balance sheet: Inventory–FBA, Inventory–In Transit, Amazon Receivables (reserves are an asset, not an expense).

**Method:** accrual accounting effectively required (bi-weekly settlements never align with sale dates; cash-basis makes monthly P&L fiction). Recognize revenue when orders ship. Expense ads when incurred. Weighted-average COGS at full landed cost (supplier + freight + duties + prep per unit).

**Cadence:** reconcile within 5 business days of each bi-weekly settlement (15–30 min with A2X); month-end close achievable in 5 business days (their day-by-day: A2X settlement verify → bank rec → inventory/COGS rec vs Amazon reports → P&L + variance review).

**Case study:** £10M UK brand believed gross margin was 60%; after unbundling fees it was 52%, Amazon CM2 was 28% vs DTC 41% → moved £8K/mo of ad spend, +£14K/mo contribution within 90 days. Wrong books = wrong strategy.

**Upgrade path:** <$1M rev: Link My Books + DIY; $1–3M: A2X + ecommerce bookkeeper; $3M+: pro bookkeeping + fractional CFO; NetSuite territory ~$30M multi-entity.
