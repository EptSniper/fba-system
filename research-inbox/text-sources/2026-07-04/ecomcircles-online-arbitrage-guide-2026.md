## Distilled content

**2026 reality check.** Realistic OA net margins are 10–20% after all fees — far below the 50%+ claims common online. 165,000 new sellers registered in 2025 (down 44% from 2024), but surviving sellers capture more traffic per seller (up 31% since 2021): less competition IF you operate systematically.

**2026 rule changes it asserts (verify officially):**
- Commingling ends March 31, 2026 — manufacturer barcodes required.
- Amazon's own FBA prep services ended January 1, 2026 — budget 3PL prep at $0.50–$2/unit.
- FBA fees increased $0.08/unit.
- Retail receipts no longer work for ungating — wholesale invoices or Letters of Authorization required. Account lockout risk if you source restricted brands without documentation.

**OA vs RA:** OA startup capital $500–$2K, net margin 10–20%, capital recycles slowly (45–90 days); RA recycles in 7–14 days. OA scales faster; RA cashflows faster. Many sellers run both early.

**Deal discipline:** never pursue deals under 40% gross margin (their example: $11.45 total COGS incl. inbound + prep + 3% inspection loss vs $39.99 sale → 42% net after referral + FBA + storage). Total COGS must include inbound shipping, 3PL prep, inspection loss (2–5%), storage.

**Six profitability killers:** miscalculated COGS; unvetted suppliers (counterfeit/FBA rejection); ignoring 2026 gating documentation rules; ordering volume before testing (start 20–50 units, verify sell-through + BSR <30K, scale only at 10+ units/week); single-marketplace listing (they push Amazon + Walmart dual-listing — Walmart WFS fees are lower on the same product); no repricing.

**Scaling roadmap:** Phase 1 validation $1–3K/mo (20–30 unit tests, 3–5 suppliers); Phase 2 $3–10K/mo (100–200 units/SKU on winners); Phase 3 $10–30K/mo (5–8 core SKUs, 80/20); Phase 4 wholesale transition $30K+/mo. Working capital: $2–5K → $10–20K → $30–50K by phase.
