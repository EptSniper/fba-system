# Sourcing Crayola: how to find winners in that brand efficiently

Good pick. Crayola is on your **friendly brand list** (durable, steady demand, no private-label lock), and it's the literal worked example in your Keepa Product Finder playbook (~8,600 listings). It's also low-cost art/craft supplies, so your job is to filter ~8,600 ASINs down to a short list of real OA winners fast, then verify each in SellerAmp.

Here's the efficient funnel, in order.

---

## Step 0 — Pre-flight checks (2 minutes, save hours)

- **Gating:** Crayola is usually ungateable, but confirm *before* you buy. While you stalk listings, paste ASINs into **Boxem's bulk ungate checker** to instantly see what's auto-ungated for your account. Rule: **list before you buy** — if an ASIN is hard-gated in Seller Central, skip it.
- **IP risk:** Run **IP Alert** (Chrome extension) on listings. Crayola isn't a notorious IP-complaint brand, but check the **brand-on-listing** signal — if Crayola is itself a seller on the ASIN, they can kick resellers off. Pass those.
- **FBA eligibility quirk for this brand:** Crayola = art supplies. Watch for **markers/paint/glue** that can be **hazmat or meltable** (some markers/paints ship as hazmat; crayons/Model Magic can be heat-sensitive). Verify FBA eligibility on anything liquid, aerosol, or wax-heavy before committing.

### Method B — Reverse sourcing / "storefront stalking" (best for finding the *retail source* too)
Other sellers already did the research on Crayola — copy them:

1. Open any name-brand Crayola listing -> **SellerAmp Offers** tab -> see every seller.
2. Click sellers with **1-100 reviews** (regular people at your level; 100-200+ = advanced, harder to copy).

Use **both**: Method A finds the ASINs, Method B finds who's already winning them and where they buy.

## Read the Keepa chart on each survivor (the core skill)

On the 3-month + 1-year views, a **winner** looks like:

- **Yellow "sold" line present** (e.g. "50 sold", "100 sold") -> sells >=50/mo. **No yellow line => under 50/mo => usually pass.** This is your fastest single filter.
- **Buy Box price (pink): stable or trending up** -> resale price is trustworthy.
- **Offer count: flat or declining** = sellers selling through (good). **Rising = avoid** — price is about to tank.
- **Lots of oscillation** (Buy Box + offers wiggling) = selling fast; long flat "blocky" stretches = slow mover.
- **Buy Box spread across many FBA sellers** = healthy. One seller or **Amazon holding ~80-100%** = skip.
- **Sellers on the listing for months** = it's been reliably profitable.

**Instant-reject red flags:** rising offer count - **price spike** (current price well above its 3-12 month history; it reverts, and FBA's ~2-week delay makes that brutal) - **seller-count spike** (race to the bottom) - **IP "cliff"** (seller count crashes e.g. 56->1 and never recovers) - Amazon owns ~100% Buy Box - no Buy Box / featured offer - hard-gated.

## Run the SellerAmp math on the keepers

For each surviving Crayola ASIN, confirm against your criteria:

- **Eligible / ungated** (SellerAmp panel tells you).
- **ROI >= 30%** after all fees and **>= $3 profit/unit.** Crayola items are cheap, so $3/unit and 30% ROI is the real hurdle — thin margins are common here, so use cost engineering below.
- **Sell price $8-$60** (your price band).
- **True landed cost** — include shipping. A $10 item with $9.95 shipping is a **$20** cost; enter the real landed number.
- Use **Max Cost** as a reverse calculator: the most you can pay and still hit 30% ROI. That's your buy-trigger price at the retailer.

### Make thin Crayola deals profitable ("manufacturing margin")
Crayola rarely shows up already profitable — you *make* it profitable by stacking:
- **Coupons** (Coupert), **cashback** (compare via RevROI: TopCashback / Rakuten / BeFrugal), **discounted gift cards** (cardbear.com, Target Circle), **Subscribe & Save** (~5-15%), and a **tax-exempt/reseller certificate** (removes sales tax at Home Depot / Lowe's / Walmart).
- Don't bake cashback into COGS — use it as a cushion on top of an already-passing deal.
- Buy from **brand sites / major retailers only** — never eBay or AliExpress (counterfeit risk).

**Where to buy Crayola:** Target (also the best ungating source), Walmart, Michaels, Staples, Office Depot, Walgreens — but the best margins usually come from **lowkey lesser-known sites** with a sale on *today*, not Walmart/Target where everyone's watching. **Start where the deal is today** (a craft BOGO or high cashback right now beats grinding a store with no sale). Q4 / back-to-school are strong seasonal windows for art supplies — check the 1-year BSR for seasonality.

## Size the buy

- **Beginner:** 5-10 units per product until you see how it actually moves.
- **Worst-case rule:** only buy where, even at the lowest historical Buy-Box price, you'd break even or lose <= $1-2/unit.
- Philosophy: **base hits, not home runs** — most products make $100-200/mo. Build a **wide Crayola catalog, then go deep on the few that sell through.** Fast nickel beats a slow dime.

1. Keepa Product Finder: Brand=Crayola, BSR 0-200k, Amazon OOS, offers min 4-5 -> ~430 candidates.

6. Buy 5-10 units, log the lead, record the outcome.
