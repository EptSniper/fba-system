## Distilled takeaways

- **[practitioner]** High-restriction categories in 2026 are consistently: Beauty & Personal Care, Dietary Supplements, Health & Personal Care, Grocery & Gourmet Food, Electronics accessories (batteries/wireless), Jewelry, Medical Devices, and Textiles (esp. children's sleepwear). Restrictions stem from safety, counterfeit, and regulatory risk — the same drivers behind Amazon's IP enforcement. Screen category gating before sourcing, not after purchase.
- **[practitioner]** Approval documents are category-specific and non-generic: supplements need an ISO/IEC 17025 lab CoA dated within ~6 months; electronics need FCC ID / UL listing; food needs FDA registration + 50+ days shelf life; medical devices need FDA 510(k). A generic distributor invoice alone is usually not enough for these gates.
- **[practitioner]** Realistic ungating timeline is ~1 week to 1 month, with an estimated ~20–35 hours of work over three weeks (assess/gather docs → apply → prep listings). Budget for it; it is not instant.
- **[practitioner]** Ungating cost ranges roughly $500 (simple) to $2,000+ (health/electronics compliance testing). Listing restricted items without approval risks inventory loss, account suspension, or a permanent ban — the downside is account-level, not just a lost sale.
- **[practitioner]** Category status can change (new complaints, recalls, counterfeit reports), so keep a documentation backup plan even for categories you're currently ungated in.
