## Cleaned content (fetched text, boilerplate removed)

Third-party arbitrage sellers face a challenge that intensified dramatically over the last few years. Amazon fixed operational headaches (inbound shipping, financial reports, analytics) but sourcing became exponentially harder due to increased competition and stricter compliance enforcement.

**Why sourcing got harder — two forces:**
1. Competition increased: more OA courses, more sellers chasing the same products. "Easy finds" that took minutes now take an hour+ manually; sessions that yielded 10 testable products now yield 2-3.
2. Amazon's compliance crackdown: GS1 barcode enforcement, expanding brand gating, GTIN validation. Example: Trader Joe's GTIN validation issues in October 2025 — half of some sellers' inventory flagged/blocked at check-in through no fault of their own.

**Cost math (theirs):** old-school manual sourcing ≈ 40 hrs/mo ≈ $1,000 time value → ~80 good leads/mo. Modern automated sourcing ≈ 4 hrs/mo + $69 tool ≈ $169/mo → 2,000+ leads/mo ("25X results for 1/6 the cost"). "The cure to almost every arbitrage problem is to test more ASINs — but you can't test more ASINs if you can't find them fast enough."

**The three-punch combo:**
- Punch 1 — Keepa storefront stalking at scale: scan up to 50 competitor storefront IDs at once, extract ALL active ASINs (limited only by Keepa quota). Goal is a database of ASINs to investigate, not "proven winners" yet. 15-30 min.
- Punch 2 — ArbiSource reverse scanning: upload the ASIN list; it scans 240+ online retail stores and returns which stores carry the products, retail prices, estimated profitability after fees, stock availability. 5-10 min.
- Punch 3 — Custom retail store scan: target ANY online store (even outside the 240), with filters (price ranges, categories, sales-rank thresholds); no coding needed. 10-20 min per scan.

**The compounding advantage — lead databases:** save every ASIN sourced, even non-buys. Products become testable in 2-6 months when retail pricing changes, stock shifts, competitors drop off, gated items become ungated, OOS items return. Rescan the database later ("shopping from the back of the list") instead of starting from zero. "Old school sourcing doesn't build databases. It builds burnout."

**Getting started:** pick one workflow; start with 3-5 storefront IDs then scale to 50; run the scan; filter by sales velocity, price points, offer counts, sales rank; test 10-12 products; repeat.
