# 02 · The Ways to Sell on Amazon (and which fits me)

*Goal: pick a starting path on purpose, not by accident.*

---

## The five main models at a glance

| Model | What it is | Startup capital | Time to 1st sale | Main risk | Beginner fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Online Arbitrage (OA)** | Buy discounted products from online stores, resell on Amazon | **$500–$2k** inventory + ~$80/mo tools | Days–weeks | IP complaints, gating, thin margins | ★★★★★ |
| **Retail Arbitrage (RA)** | Same, but buy in physical stores (clearance) | $300–$1k | Days | Doesn't scale, time-heavy | ★★★★☆ |
| **Wholesale** | Buy established brands in bulk from distributors, resell | $2k–$10k+ | Weeks–months | Getting accounts, competition | ★★★☆☆ |
| **Private Label (PL)** | Create your own brand/product, manufacture, own the listing | **$2.5k–$5k+** | 3–6 months | Big upfront bet on one product | ★★☆☆☆ |
| **Dropshipping** | List items you don't own; supplier ships on order | Low | — | **Mostly against Amazon policy** | ★☆☆☆☆ |

A note on **dropshipping**: Amazon's dropshipping policy *prohibits* buying from
another retailer (e.g. Walmart) and having them ship directly to your Amazon
customer. Legit dropshipping (you're the seller of record, your own supplier, your
packing slips) is allowed but hard and low-margin. **Skip it.**

---

## How each model really feels

**Online & Retail Arbitrage** — You're a *reseller* of products that already sell
on Amazon. You add your offer to an existing listing and compete for the Buy Box.
The whole game is **finding price gaps**: a product selling for $30 on Amazon that
you can buy for $14 elsewhere, where the math clears profit after fees. Low
capital, fast feedback, and you learn every core skill (Seller Central, FBA
shipping, fees, gating, Buy Box) on real money without betting the farm. Downsides:
it's a **sourcing grind** (you sift many products to find a few), margins are
thinner, you can get **brand-gated** or hit **IP complaints**, and it doesn't build
a brand asset. **This is where tools like Keepa + SellerAmp SAS pay for themselves**
— they turn the grind into a fast, repeatable check.

**Wholesale** — Like arbitrage but you buy in bulk directly from brands/distributors
with real invoices (which also makes ungating easier). More stable and scalable than
arbitrage, but you need to open accounts, often hit minimums, and compete with other
sellers on the same listings. A natural step *up* from arbitrage.

**Private Label** — You make your *own* product and own the listing outright — no
Buy Box competition. Highest ceiling, builds a sellable brand asset, but it's a
bigger upfront bet (inventory + photos + launch + ads) on a single product, and
takes months to validate. **This is what `01_research_brief.md` and the `scout`
system in this folder are built for** — they're the upgrade path, not the day-one path.

---

## My recommendation: start with Online Arbitrage

Given exactly where I am, OA is the clear starting point:

1. **I have a mentor in it.** My dad's friend has done online arbitrage since 2017.
   A live expert to ask is worth more than any course — and it's model-specific
   help I can actually use.
2. **Lowest capital, lowest risk.** ~$500–$2,000 of inventory + ~$80/mo of tools
   (Seller Central $40, Keepa $20, SellerAmp $20) vs. $2.5k–$5k for private label.
3. **Fastest real learning.** I'll touch every core skill — listings, FBA prep &
   shipping, fee math, gating/ungating, Buy Box, returns — within weeks, on small
   stakes.
4. **The tool stack is already OA-shaped.** Keepa + SellerAmp SAS are the standard
   OA sourcing kit, and the `scout` AI in this folder already speaks Keepa.

**Then graduate.** Once I can consistently find profitable deals and have cash
flow, the path is OA → (optionally wholesale) → **private label**, where the brief
and scout system unlock their full value and I build a brand I could one day sell.

> **Honest caveats.** OA is not passive and not guaranteed. It's a real grind, margins
> are thinner than PL, and brand gating / IP complaints are constant friction. The
> point of starting here isn't that it's easy money — it's that it's the **cheapest,
> fastest, lowest-risk classroom**, with a mentor and the right tools already lined up.
