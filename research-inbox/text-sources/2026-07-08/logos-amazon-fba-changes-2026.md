## Why it matters for this project
Corroborates and dates the "sourcing is harder / margins thinner in 2026" thread already staged, and flags
operational changes (prep-service end, tighter storage) that affect OA unit economics and cash-flow planning.

## Key takeaways
1. **Another FBA fee increase for 2026** — average ~**+$0.08/unit** per industry reporting; small per unit,
   material at volume, pushes thin-margin SKUs toward breakeven.
2. **Amazon ended US FBA prep & labeling services (Jan 1 2026)** — FNSKU labeling, poly-bagging, bubble-
   wrap, bundling, compliance prep are now the **seller's or a 3PL's responsibility** before inventory
   reaches the FC. Directly relevant to OA: every purchased unit must be prepped upstream now.
3. **Storage limits tightened (mid-2025): ~6 → ~5 months** of forecasted sales — more frequent restocking,
   higher Q4 / Prime-Day stockout and congestion risk.
4. **Reimbursement now valued at sourcing cost, not selling price** (March-2025 change; sellers can supply
   own cost data or accept Amazon's estimate) — same shift the Nova reimbursement guide details; reinforces
   accurate COGS records.
5. **Platform-dependency risk** — a Dec-2025 regional listing-hide incident is used to argue for
   diversifying fulfilment/sales channels (FBM, Shopify, wholesale) as a hedge.
