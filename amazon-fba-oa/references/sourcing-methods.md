# Sourcing methods — shared reference

Distilled from `learning-hub/playbooks/sourcing-playbook.md` and the live-sourcing transcripts.
Read the full playbook for detail; this is the quick operational map.

## Deal-first principle (start here)

Don't grind a store that has no sale. Source where the best **sale / cashback / coupon / BOGO is TODAY**,
then stack discounts to manufacture margin a competitor without the stack can't match. "Manufacturing
margin": you rarely find a deal already profitable — you *make* it profitable by stacking.

Stack levers: signup/coupon codes (Coupert), cashback (compare via RevROI: TopCashback, Rakuten, BeFrugal),
discounted gift cards (cardbear.com, Target Circle), Subscribe & Save (~5–15%), military/student/nurse/
veteran discounts, tax-exempt reseller cert, free-shipping thresholds. Buy from brand sites / major
retailers only — never eBay or AliExpress (counterfeit risk). Don't bake cashback into COGS — treat it as cushion.

## Method A — Reverse sourcing / storefront stalking (best for beginners)

1. On a name-brand listing, open SellerAmp **Offers** → see every seller.
2. Click sellers with **1–100 reviews** (regular people, copyable; 100–200+ = advanced).
3. Their **storefront** = vetted OA leads. Analyze each; find the retail source via the Google button.
4. Go down rabbit holes — each storefront reveals brands, each brand reveals sellers. Save winning seller IDs.
5. Tell a seller is OA: they carry name brands (Nike, Jellycat, Yeti, Crocs), not private label. Income ≈ #ASINs × ~$50/mo.

## Method B — Keepa Product Finder (scalable, when you know a brand)

keepa.com → Data → Product Finder. Brand = target. Sales Rank 0 → 150,000–200,000 (top ~1%).
Amazon: out of stock. New offer count: min 4–5. Optionally sort by 90-day drop % to surface spike windows. Analyze survivors.

## Method C — Manual sourcing (only on sites you know well)

Browse clearance/sale pages live. Slow; not for beginners. Favor mainstream colors (black/white/gray), skip odd colors.

## Sizing the buy

- Beginner: 5–10 units/product until you know how it moves.
- Formula: `variation monthly sales ÷ (price-competitive sellers + you)`, then cut 30–50%.
- Worst case: only buy if, at the lowest historical Buy-Box price, you break even or lose ≤ $1–2/unit.

## Anti-flag tactics when ordering

Small first orders, pay with PayPal/Apple/Google Pay, or message customer service to lift reseller flags.
List the ASIN in Seller Central **before** you buy — if it's hard-gated, don't buy. See `oa-criteria.md` for gates.
