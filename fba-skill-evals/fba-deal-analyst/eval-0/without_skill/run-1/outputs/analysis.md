# OA Deal Analysis — B07XYZ1234 (Crayola Marker Set)

## Verdict: DO NOT BUY at these numbers (the profit isn't there)

The sourcing, demand, and stability all look fine. The problem is the math: at a $20 all-in cost into a $29.99 sale, the FBA fees eat almost the entire spread. This is a **break-even-to-loss deal**, not a clean buy.

### What this means
- **Best case** (lowest FBA fee): about **$0.84 profit per unit, ~4% ROI**.
- **Realistic/worst case** (higher FBA fee, which is likely for a boxed marker set): you **lose money** on every sale.

A standard OA buy target is roughly **30%+ ROI and $3+ net profit per unit** to be worth the cash, time, and risk. This deal is nowhere close. One return, one damaged unit, or one fee you didn't model and you're underwater.

## So how many should you buy? — Zero, for now

Don't place a buy at $20 all-in. But the product itself is worth keeping on your list, because the demand and stability profile is genuinely good:

The bottleneck is 100% cost, not demand.

## How to make this an actual buy

Re-run it if any of these change:

1. **Lower your cost.** You need landed cost around **$13–14** to hit a real margin. Look for:
   - Target Circle / RedCard 5% + a coupon or Circle deal
   - A multi-pack or larger Crayola set with better unit economics
   - Buying enough to pull free shipping (kill the $4 ship — that alone is the difference between a loss and ~$4 profit/unit)
2. **Confirm the fees before buying anything.** Plug the exact ASIN into the **Amazon Revenue Calculator** (or your Keepa/SellerAmp FBA fee field). The FBA fulfillment fee is the swing variable here — get the real number, don't trust my $4.25–$5.50 estimate.
3. **Check for hazmat/gating.** Markers can occasionally flag as hazmat (solvent-based) and Crayola/Toys can be gated or have Q4 brand restrictions. Verify you can list it before sourcing.

---

## If the cost drops and it pencils out

Once you confirm exact FBA fees and hit ~30% ROI / $3+ profit:
- Start with a **test buy of 10–15 units** (about 1–2 weeks of your expected buy-box share), not a full month.
- Confirm it actually sells through at your price and that you win buy-box rotation.
- Then scale to a **3–4 week supply (~25–50 units)** on the reorder once velocity is proven.

Never load up a full month of inventory on the first buy of a product you haven't sold before — prove the rotation first, then scale.

Good product, good demand, good stability — **bad cost basis.** Pass at $20 all-in. Get landed cost to ~$13–14 (kill the shipping, stack a discount), re-verify the exact FBA fee, and if it clears ~30% ROI / $3+ profit, start with a 10–15 unit test before scaling to a few weeks' supply.
