## Distilled content

**Method (confirms staged material):** start from reseller-friendly brands (Nike, Trader Joe's, Kirkland — ~90% of the time carried by other resellers) → open those sellers' storefronts → reverse-source where to buy at profit → validate with Keepa (rank, sell frequency, price history) → find suppliers via search.

**What makes a GOOD storefront to stalk (the useful part — concrete screen):**
- Mixed-category product variety (sellers who spread across categories tend to know what sells)
- **No more than ~1,000 products** in the storefront
- Irregular, smaller-batch listing patterns = a small reseller who researches each item (better leads)
- Amazon NOT competing directly on their listings
- Products similar to what you already sell (comparable niche)
- AVOID storefronts with huge single-brand inventories — that's wholesale, not stalkable OA

**Freshness angle:** monitoring newly-listed products in target storefronts (their tool's pitch) — new listings are the highest-signal leads before they're crowded.
