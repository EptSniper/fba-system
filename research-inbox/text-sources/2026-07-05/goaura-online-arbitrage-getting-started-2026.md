## Content (condensed)

### Why OA still works in 2026
Marketplace Pulse 2025 Year-in-Review framing: Amazon is now an operational-excellence game;
sophisticated tools are requirements, not advantages. Casual sellers left; systematic arbitrage
sellers (automated scans, repricers, VAs, prep centers) are who the market favors. Opportunities
persist because retail markets are inherently inefficient (clearance cycles, promo calendars,
information asymmetry, execution barriers).

### Five sourcing strategies (use 2–3 together)
1. **Manual deal hunting** — browse clearance/flash sales; 20–50 product checks per session builds
   pattern recognition. Free but time-heavy.
2. **Automated list scanning** — Tactical Arbitrage / SourceMogul with ROI + BSR filters ($50–100+/mo).
3. **Reverse sourcing** — start from proven Amazon ASINs / successful sellers' storefronts, work
   backward to retail sources. Higher hit rate; needs more skill.
4. **VA sourcing** — $4–8/hr, train on your buying criteria; frees 15–25 hrs/week; quality varies.
5. **Lead lists** — $50–200+/mo; instant vetted deals but shared with other subscribers, tighter margins.

### Numbers repeatedly cited
- Starting inventory capital: **$250–800** for new sellers; Professional account $39.99/mo.
- Common buy floor: **$3+ profit, 30% ROI, BSR under 100k** (pros tighten to $4 / 35%).
- Typical realized profits: **10–15% per item** (vs inflated claims elsewhere).
- Fees typically 30–40% of the sale price; break-even usually months 2–3.
- Buy Box captures ~82% of sales (FBA strongly favored).

### Inventory management rules
- **3-month rule**: only buy what sells in ~90 days (OA often can't restock anyway).
- Velocity-based buying: rank history consistently <100k, steady Keepa demand (not one-off spikes),
  FBA seller count + typical Buy Box price, seasonality timing.
- Storage math sellers ignore: standard $0.75–2.40/cu ft/mo; long-term (365d+) $6.90/cu ft or
  $0.15/unit; aged surcharge 271–365d $1.50–4.30/cu ft.
- Removal cadence: reassess price at 30 days, consider removal at 60, remove before long-term fees at 90.
- KPIs: IPI > 450, sell-through rate, days of inventory, stranded %, aged %.
- Hazmat is a hidden killer: beauty aerosols/nail polish, cleaners/adhesives, lithium batteries,
  battery/magnet toys — verify before buying.

### Margin stacking (profit enhancers)
Layered: cashback credit cards (2–5%) → cashback portals (Rakuten/TopCashback/BeFrugal, compare via
Cashback Monitor) → discounted gift cards (3–10% off via Raise/CardCash) → coupon extensions (Honey)
→ retailer loyalty programs (Target Circle, Walmart+). Worked example: $20→$40 product goes from
$8 profit (40% ROI) to $13.60 (68% ROI) with full stack. Plus the **30% rule**: reserve 30% of profits
for taxes/returns/fees.

### Risk management
Never >20% of capital in one ASIN; maintain 50+ listings across 5+ categories; keep 20% capital reserve
for restriction changes; diversification is survival. Race-to-zero defense: source beyond obvious
clearance, use strategic (not lowest-price) repricing. [Vendor pitch: Aura repricer claims 40–60% Buy Box
