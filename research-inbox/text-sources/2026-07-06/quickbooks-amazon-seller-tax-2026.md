## Content (condensed)

**1099-K (2026 tax year).** Reporting threshold has RETURNED to the historical standard: Amazon issues a
1099-K only if gross sales > $20,000 AND > 200 transactions. Regardless of receiving one, all business
income must be reported to the IRS. Find it: Seller Central → Reports → Tax Document Library.

**Sales tax.** Under marketplace facilitator laws Amazon automatically collects and remits sales tax in
nearly every state. Two things still on the seller: (1) **nexus** — FBA inventory placement can create
PHYSICAL nexus in the ~44 states/DC with Amazon warehouses; ECONOMIC nexus triggers at state thresholds
(often $100k sales) and requires registering for permits even with no physical presence; (2) some states
still require zero-dollar/informational returns even when Amazon remitted the tax. Reports for this:
Marketplace Tax Collection Report (what Amazon paid for you) vs Sales Tax Calculation Report (what you
were responsible for).

**Product taxability.** Set product tax codes correctly — groceries/medicine/some apparel are exempt or
reduced-rate in many states, and Amazon will overcharge if codes are wrong. Resellers can use resale
certificates to avoid paying sales tax on inventory purchases (directly relevant to OA buy cost).

**Income tax.** Sole prop / single-member LLC → Schedule C with the personal 1040. Deductions that lower
taxable income: shipping, home office, software/tool fees (Keepa, SellerAmp subscriptions qualify as
business software). Local business-license requirements may apply even though Amazon doesn't ask.
