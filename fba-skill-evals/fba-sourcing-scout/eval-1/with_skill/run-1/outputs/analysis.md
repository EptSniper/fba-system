## Flag the obvious instant-rejects EARLY (Crayola-specific)

So you don't waste analyst time, kill these on sight before deep analysis:
- **Amazon holds the Buy Box** (now or ≥20% historically) → hard reject. Amazon stocks core Crayola directly a lot — this is the #1 Crayola killer.
- **Price spike:** current price > 1.5× 90-day avg → reverts; brutal with FBA's ~2-week delay (common on seasonal Crayola).
- **Rising offers:** new-offer count > 1.4× 90-day avg → seller pile-in, price about to tank (very common on basic crayon/marker packs everyone can find).
- **IP cliff:** offers crash and never recover (e.g. 56 → 1) → brand filed complaints.
- **Too-easy-to-find / commodity:** the 24-count crayon box at every store with 40 sellers → skip; no moat.
- **Seasonal traps:** back-to-school and holiday Crayola kits — fine to ride the wave, but don't get stuck holding off-season inventory.
- **Sell price outside $8–$60** → out of band.

## Sizing the buy (when one clears)

- Beginner: **5–10 units/product** until you see how it moves.
- Formula: `variation monthly sales ÷ (price-competitive sellers + you)`, then **cut 30–50%**.
- Worst case: only buy if, at the lowest historical Buy-Box price, you **break even or lose ≤ $1–2/unit**.

## Anti-flag when ordering
Small first orders; pay with PayPal / Apple / Google Pay; message customer service to lift reseller flags if needed. **List the ASIN in Seller Central BEFORE you buy** — if Crayola is hard-gated on your account, don't buy.
