## Per-video detail

### 1 · Beginners Course (HJes4bs2d64)
The backbone. OA = buy name-brand products discounted from online retailers, resell on
Amazon. ~60% of Amazon sales are 3rd-party. Models: PL, wholesale, RA, OA (recommended),
dropshipping (banned — skip). Terms: FBA/FBM, Buy Box, BSR, ASIN, BOLO, prep center, VA.
Stack: Seller Central $40/mo + Keepa $20 + SellerAmp $20. Cashback/coupon extensions:
**Rakuten, TopCashback, Coupert**; **RevROI** compares cashback. Ungating: buy 10 units,
save invoice, expect 5–10 tries. No LLC needed for OA (seller's permit only for wholesale).
Margins ~10–15%; volume higher than PL. **Keepa reading is the core skill** (Buy Box price
stability, offer-count trend, BSR/seasonality, Buy-Box rotation, historical offers). Bad-product
red flags: rising offer count (price will tank), IP cliff (56→1 sellers = brand IP complaint =
account-health hit), Amazon owning ~100% Buy Box, no Buy Box. Test 5–10 units → go deep on
winners; wide catalog. FBM first to learn faster (he did FBM 6–8 months). Outsource later
(prep center ~$1.50/unit; VAs $3–5/hr).

### 2 · The $100 Start (A9JIrrL6AaI) — Thomas
Start OA with ~$100, **FBM not FBA** (faster learning, faster payouts, no storage/inbound fees;
end goal 99–100% FBA once mastered). Product sweet spot for FBM: **lightweight, ~$10–15, small,
not fragile**, fits a poly mailer/small box. **#1 beginner category = grocery** — cheap, easy to
ship, and **groceries can't be returned on Amazon → profit basically guaranteed**; easy to ungate
via target.com (buy 10 of the cheapest grocery item, submit invoice). Software: just SellerAmp
(14-day free trial → make a sale before it ends → use profit to pay the $20). Lean supplies: buy
boxes one at a time (Walmart/Staples, not bulk Uline), free UPS shipping labels (up to ~1,000),
use a **library printer** until you profit. **Diversify** (1 unit here/there); leverage retailer
return windows while testing so unsold tests don't lose money. Data over vibes.

### 3 · Ungating Master Guide (cAKdhzKFIzE)
Three product types: **(a) auto-ungated** — sellable immediately; more sales → Amazon trusts you →
more auto-ungates (focus here first; e.g. Stafford, Cuddle Duds, many Walmart items). **(b) gated,
ungate via invoice** — most common; requires 10/50/100/200 units depending on brand. **(c) hard-gated**
— can't even apply (Nike, Adidas); only grandfathered sellers. Always click "Apply to sell / Request
approval" — sometimes it instantly approves (hidden auto-ungate). You do **not** need a wholesaler;
buy direct from the brand site or a known-good site. Amazon wants a document that says **"Invoice"**
and shows your shipping address + item name (not an "order confirmation"). **Best beginner ungating
site = target.com** (receipts say Invoice; ~$80 for 10 units ungated Crayola). Expect many tries
(5–50). **Watch for instant bot denial** (denied <5–10 min = auto-filter, no chance) → cooldown
24–48h, try a different ASIN, or reply to the case. Boost approval odds by submitting a **photo of
the 10 units laid out** + renaming the PDF. **Ungate "free"**: buy 10, get ungated, return within
Target's 30–60 day window (don't abuse). **Don't pay ungating services** ($500+) — do it yourself.

### 4 · Q4 / Seasonal Guide (IqpFt2AKPr0)
Q4 ≈ where the money is (claims $1M sales / $200k profit in a Q4). 10 rules: (1) Don't over-index on
seasonal items — **double down on existing winners** (his profit came from 2–3 brands, e.g. Stanley).
(2) Stock up on shipping supplies *before* the rush. (3) Limited capital → buy fast-selling profitable
items only. **Q4 criteria: BSR 0–200k, ≥$3 profit/unit, ROI 30%+, Keepa stable/increasing price +
stable/decreasing competition + good all-time profitability.** (4) Favor **storefront stalking**;
don't test new sites in Q4. (Cautionary: $80k on Lululemon → orders canceled, money held 3 weeks.)
(5) **Don't send FBA shipments after ~Nov 15** (need them checked in to catch demand). (6) FBM-heavy
lets you list bundles (2/3/4-packs) + alternate listings; don't oversell. (7) **Premium-shipping
profit** — set expedited $25–30 / 2-day $40–50 / 1-day $60–80; he made ~$5k on shipping labels alone.
(8) Network (Discord/social). (9) **Don't speculate on price increases** — buy profitable-now; rises
are a bonus. (10) "Make products good" — stack tax-exempt, coupons, cashback, discounted gift cards,
rewards, holiday sales, bulk programs to lower cost.

### 5 · SellerAmp Full Tutorial (G_pGhjkWNY4)
Plans: **Getting Started** (1,000 lookups/mo, ~$19.95) vs **Getting Serious** (unlimited, ~$27.95);
start with Getting Started, upgrade at 1,000 lookups; annual saves. **Settings to copy:** inbound

**min profit $3**, **min ROI 30%** (returns), prep fee if any, sales tax baked into cost price (not
the % field). Panel fields: title, dimensions (FBM cost), Google button (sourcing), ASIN (add to
catalog), **eligibility** (gated?), alerts (Amazon-on-BuyBox / private-label / IP / oversized /
variations), BSR, **estimated sales** (Amazon's number, accurate), **Max Cost** (max to pay to hit
your ROI — usable as a reverse calculator), **break-even price**, **offers** (# sellers + FBA/FBM
split), Keepa data (30/90/180-day BSR, Buy Box, lowest FBA/FBM, price drops). Profit calculator
toggles FBA↔FBM. **Google Sheets export** → keep a "sourcing" sheet + a "buy" sheet (VAs use these).
"Use every data point, not one — that separates good sellers from bad." Manual sourcing NOT for

products). Sellers with **1–100 reviews = regular people (replicable)**; 100–200+ = advanced. Estimate
a competitor's income: # ASINs × ~$50 min. Avoid: Amazon-on-listing at low prices, dead Keepa charts,

### 7 · Back-to-School Live + Q&A (QULKMGY6iqc) — **Q&A gold**
**Puma is ungateable** (unlike Nike/Adidas) → good beginner brand; slides + generic colors sell.
**Per-variation sales estimate:** SellerAmp's estimate is for the *whole* listing; to size a single
size/color, open **variations → sort by ratings**, take that variation's **review % × total estimated
sales**. Buy the higher-selling variations (top 1–2%). **Units-to-buy formula:** *variation monthly
sales ÷ (price-competitive sellers + you)* = units you can capture; then cut 30–50% to be conservative.
Beginner rule: **5–10 units/product** until you know it sells. **"Base hits, not home runs"** — most
products make ~$100–200/mo each; only ~10–20 of his ~200–250 make $1,000+/mo. **Worst case** (if you
buy at Keepa lows) = break-even or −$1–2/unit; you can remove FBA inventory / liquidate on eBay. Every
product has a **life cycle** (3–6 mo to 1–2 yr); evergreen is rare; deals reach price equilibrium as
they spread. **Buy Box:** FBA gets priority (Amazon favors 2-day shipping), but a very competitive FBM
price can win it; the two factors are **price + fulfillment speed**. Coupons: **Coupert**; **RevROI**
to compare cashback (TopCashback consistent; Rakuten; BeFrugal sometimes 10%). Avoid sourcing
Target/Walmart heavily (too many eyes) — find **"lowkey websites."** Kohl's Cash loop mentioned.

### 8 · Back-to-School LIVE Research (TKLHQt_1w_Q) — **Keepa Product Finder**
New method: **keepa.com → Data → Product Finder.** Filter **Brand** (e.g. Crayola = 8,619 listings) +
**Sales Rank 0 → 150,000–200,000** (top ~1%, drops to ~1,000) + **Amazon: out of stock** (don't compete
with Amazon; −~300) + **New offer count ≥ 4–5** (ensures OA competition, not single-seller/wholesale) →
~430 listings. Optional: sort by **90-day drop %** to find items priced well above their 90-day average
(price-spike windows, e.g. current $20 vs avg $11 = +76%). Then analyze each in SellerAmp/Keepa.
Seasonal demand makes normally-unprofitable items profitable; thin per-unit profit ($2–2.50) is fine at
high volume. "50 products × $100/mo = $5k."

### 9 · Sourcing Marathon (V6xFrt3pcC8)

## Consolidated OA system (the repeatable loop)

1. **Source candidates** — reverse sourcing (open sellers' storefronts via SellerAmp → their other brands)
   and/or **Keepa Product Finder** (brand + BSR<200k + Amazon OOS + offers≥4–5). Manual sourcing only on
   sites you know.
2. **Read the Keepa chart** — Buy Box price stable/up; offer count flat/down (rising = avoid); BSR healthy +
   check 1-yr seasonality; Buy Box rotating among many FBA sellers (not one seller / Amazon at ~90–100%);
   yellow "sold" line present (≥50/mo); oscillation = fast, blocky = slow; good all-time history.
3. **Run the SellerAmp math** — eligible/ungated; ROI ≥30% (≥25% for non-returnable grocery) after fees;
   ≥$3 profit/unit; account true landed cost (incl. shipping). Use coupons/cashback/gift-card stacking to
   close marginal deals.
4. **Size the buy** — beginner 5–10 units; advanced = variation monthly sales ÷ (competitive sellers + you),
   then −30–50%. Worst case should be break-even.
5. **List & fulfill** — FBM first (fast learning/cash flow), graduate to FBA. Bundles on FBM. Don't oversell.
6. **Record outcome** → label good/bad → trains the scout. Reinvest; double down on winners (wide catalog,
   go deep on hits). "Base hits, not home runs."
