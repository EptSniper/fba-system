# Amazon FBA Bookkeeping 2026: A2X vs Link My Books

Key points (distilled; full article at source URL):

## The "Deposit Trap"
- Seller Central "Sales" ≠ bank deposit. Amazon nets dozens of fees (shipping, ads, refunds, storage,
  reserves) before payout. Booking revenue on cash received (**cash-basis**) hides true margin — e.g. $3,000
  of ad spend deducted from your payout can make a losing SKU look healthy.
- Fix = **accrual accounting** (record the sale when it happens, not when paid). Infeasible manually at
  thousands of orders → use a "connector tool" that posts clean journal entries to QuickBooks Online / Xero.
- **DD+7 policy**: from ~March 12, 2026 Amazon holds funds 7 days after delivery. A January sale may not pay
  until February; cash-basis books then distort across the month boundary. (Cross-source claim — verify the
  exact date/mechanics against Amazon directly; treat as practitioner, not confirmed policy.)

## Tool comparison
| Tool | Method | Pricing | COGS | Inventory sync | Best for |
|------|--------|---------|------|----------------|----------|
| **A2X** | Summary journal entries | Per-channel (pricey multi-channel) | Basic | No | Accuracy purists / CPA-driven, GAAP |
| **Link My Books** | Summary (invoice) | By volume, all channels incl. (cheaper) | Advanced (auto) | No | Small/mid multi-channel, DIY |
| **Webgility** | Order-level detail | Flat/tiered (high) | Advanced | Yes | Omnichannel brands needing inventory sync |

## COGS is the missing piece
- Generic QuickBooks is poor at inventory. A2X / Link My Books let you upload a **per-SKU cost price**; when a
  unit sells the tool tells QuickBooks to reduce the inventory asset and increase COGS automatically. Without
  this you never know true monthly profit.

## OA relevance
Per-unit **landed cost as COGS + accrual basis** is the only way the scout's ROI numbers tie to reality;
the DD+7 hold is a cash-flow timing shock a tiny bankroll must plan around.
