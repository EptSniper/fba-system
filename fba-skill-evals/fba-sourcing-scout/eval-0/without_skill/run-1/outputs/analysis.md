# Brand New, $500, No Idea What to Sell — Where to Start Sourcing Today

You're in a great spot to learn the right way: a small budget forces discipline. The single biggest mistake beginners make is "product hunting" — staring at lists trying to guess a winner. Don't do that. In online arbitrage (OA) you don't invent a product; you find an *existing* Amazon listing that's already selling and buy the same item cheaper from a retail store. The deal finds you. Here's exactly how to begin today.

## First: protect the $500

That $500 is your tuition AND your inventory. Split it so one bad buy doesn't end your run:

- **Tools (~$30–60/mo, mostly free trials to start):** Keepa (~$19/mo) is non-negotiable — it's the price/rank history that tells you if a product actually sells. SellerAmp (SAS) or a free FBA calculator runs your profit math. Start both on trials.
- **First inventory (~$300–400):** Spread across **3–5 different products**, ~$50–100 each. Do NOT put $400 into one product on your first buy. You want shots on goal and lessons, not one big bet.
- **Buffer (~$50):** prep supplies, a shipment to Amazon, the odd return.

Sell-price target for a beginner: **$15–$40 items**. Cheap enough that $50–100 buys a real test quantity, expensive enough that fees don't eat all the profit.

## The numbers a deal must hit (your gates)

Before you fall in love with anything, it has to clear all of these. If it misses one, pass — there's always another deal.

| Gate | Beginner target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **ROI** | >= 30% net (after ALL Amazon fees) | Your margin of safety on a small budget |
| **Profit per unit** | >= $3–5 minimum | Below this, one return wipes the batch |
| **BSR (sales rank)** | Strong & stable in its category (e.g. top ~1–2%) | Proves it actually sells, repeatedly |
| **Sales velocity** | Sells enough/month to clear your units in ~30–60 days | Don't tie up cash in slow movers |
| **Sellers on listing** | Roughly 3–15 FBA sellers | Too few = risky/IP; too many = price war |
| **Buy Box price stability** | Flat-ish on Keepa, no nosedive | A falling price line = your profit evaporates |

Run the math on the *net* number SellerAmp/the calculator gives you, never the sticker gap.

## Sourcing TODAY — the deal-first method (do this in order)

**Step 1 — Open the sales, not the product lists.**
Go straight to the clearance / sale / coupon pages of big retailers that ship cheap and have lots of Amazon-matched inventory:
- Walmart, Target, Kohl's, Macy's clearance
- Home Depot / Lowe's, Walgreens / CVS, Big Lots
- Manufacturer outlet and "last chance" pages
Start at the **biggest current discount** (50%+ off, stacked coupons, cashback). Deep discounts are where margin lives.

**Step 2 — Scan a sale page with a calculator extension.**
Install the Keepa and SellerAmp browser extensions. As you scroll a clearance page, the extension shows you the matching Amazon listing's price, rank, and rough profit inline — so you can skim past the junk fast and stop only on items that *might* clear your gates.

**Step 3 — Storefront stalking (reverse sourcing).**
Find a successful seller in a category and look at the products *they* sell, then trace those back to a cheaper retail source. You're reverse-engineering proven winners instead of guessing. Pair this with **Keepa Product Finder** later to filter the whole catalog by BSR, price, and seller count.

**Step 4 — Verify every candidate before buying.** For each item that looks good:
1. Pull the **Keepa chart** — is the rank consistently good and the price stable? (Not a one-time spike.)
2. Run **net profit + ROI** in SellerAmp/the calculator with the real Amazon fees.
3. **Check eligibility** — can you even sell it? In Amazon Seller Central, search the product; if you're "gated" (need approval) or it's a restricted brand/hazmat/meltable item, skip it for now. As a new account you'll be ungated on fewer brands — that's normal.
4. **IP risk** — avoid major brands known to chase resellers, and anything that looks like it could draw a complaint. When unsure, pass.

Only when an item passes all four do you buy a small test quantity.

## Today's concrete checklist

2. Confirm your Amazon Seller account and the FBA workflow basics.
3. Open 3–4 retailer clearance pages and scan with the extension.
4. Collect ~10–15 *candidates* that look like they clear the gates.
5. Verify each on Keepa + profit + eligibility; keep the ones that survive.
6. Buy small test quantities of your **3–5 best**, ~$50–100 each.
7. Write down every buy (cost, expected ROI, why) so your real outcomes teach you what to source next.

## What NOT to do as a beginner

- Don't chase trendy/seasonal "hot products" — you can't read their demand history yet.
- Don't buy restricted, hazmat, meltable, or grocery/topical items until you understand the rules.
- Don't go deep on a single product. Diversify your first buys.
- Don't trust the price gap alone — only the **net** number after fees counts.
- Don't skip Keepa. A deal you can't verify on Keepa is a guess, not a deal.

## The mindset

Sourcing isn't finding "the product." It's running every candidate through the same gates until a few survive, buying those small, and letting your real sell-through tell you where to dig next time. Expect your first batch to be about *learning the process*, not getting rich. Do the loop a few times and good deals start to recognize themselves.
