## Cleaned content (fetched text, boilerplate removed)

**Why Amazon bookkeeping is complex:** Amazon holds money and deposits in biweekly settlements net of fees (referral, FBA, ads) — fees never touch the bank account, so tracking only deposits misses deductible expenses, overpays tax, and hides real profitability. Returns/refunds distort revenue if not tracked separately.

**Settlement report = the bookkeeping foundation.** Reconcile every settlement against the bank statement: gross product sales, product charges, refunds, Amazon fees (referral usually 8-15%, FBA fulfillment, monthly storage), advertising fees, other adjustments (promo rebates, A-to-Z claims), net deposit.

**Recommended chart of accounts:** Revenue (Amazon Sales; Amazon Refunds as contra-revenue). COGS (product cost; inbound shipping). OpEx (referral fees; FBA fulfillment fees; FBA storage fees; advertising; $39.99/mo Professional subscription; returns processing; other fees — LTSF, removals). Dedicated accounts per fee type make cost-structure analysis possible.

**Cash vs accrual:** cash basis is fine for most small sellers (allowed under $25M gross receipts for tax); accrual is more accurate and GAAP-required for audited financials. Under cash basis, when the deposit lands: debit Cash, then break out gross sales (credit), each fee type (debit), refunds (revenue reduction). Reconcile within days of each settlement.

**Inventory & COGS:** COGS = manufacturing/wholesale cost + import duties/customs + freight to prep/warehouse + prep/inspection (labeling, bundling). Do NOT include fulfillment fees, ads, or platform fees in COGS. Periodic (count-based) vs perpetual (real-time, software) tracking; perpetual for growing businesses.

**Sales tax:** marketplace facilitator laws mean Amazon collects/remits for most marketplace sales in most states, BUT: own-website/other-channel sales are the seller's problem; FBA inventory placement can create nexus in states you've never visited. Actions: run an inventory placement report, register where FBA-created nexus requires it, use TaxJar/Avalara for multi-state, keep records of Amazon's facilitator collections.

**FBA inventory reconciliation:** reconcile inbound sent vs received, lost/damaged reimbursements (taxable income — record them), removal orders, destroyed/disposed inventory (COGS deduction). Use the Inventory Adjustments report in Seller Central.

**Quarterly estimated taxes (US, 2026 due dates):** Apr 15, Jun 16, Sep 15, Jan 15 2027. Required if expecting to owe ≥$1,000 federal. Safe harbor: 100% of last year's liability (110% if AGI > $150k) or 90% of current-year expected.

**Common mistakes:** recording only deposits as revenue; ignoring fees as expenses; commingling personal/business finances; not tracking inventory; falling behind on reconciliation (monthly minimum, weekly for volume); forgetting reimbursements (creates 1099-K discrepancies).

**When to hire a bookkeeper:** revenue > $10-15k/mo, multi-channel, importing, >few hrs/mo on books, >1 month behind, or unsure at tax time — pick one with Amazon/ecommerce experience.

**Tools:** A2X (settlement → accounting software mapping), Fetcher, Inventory Planner, TaxJar/Avalara, or manual sheets. "Consistency matters more than sophistication."
