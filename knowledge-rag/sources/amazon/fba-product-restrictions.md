# FBA product restrictions

Non-compliance can cause refusal/disposal/return of inventory, blocked future shipments, prep or
non-compliance charges, account deactivation, or fines. Some products are sellable on Amazon but
**not eligible for FBA**.

## FBA prohibited products (do NOT send to FBA)
- **Alcoholic beverages** (including non-alcoholic beer)
- **Vehicle tires**
- **Gift cards, gift certificates, stored-value instruments**
- **Products with unauthorized marketing materials** — pamphlets, **price tags, or other non-Amazon
  stickers**. **Amazon will NOT accept pre-priced labels or products.** *(Arbitrage rule: remove or
  fully cover the retail price sticker on every unit before shipping.)*
- Products requiring prep that **weren't prepped** per FBA packaging/prep requirements
- **Loose packaged batteries**
- **Damaged or defective units** (used may be damaged if labeled appropriately)
- Products with labels **not registered with Amazon** before shipment, or mismatched
- Products that violate any Amazon–seller agreement
- **Counterfeit / illegally replicated** products (Amazon may destroy; removal can be denied)
- Anything Amazon otherwise deems unsuitable
- **Dangerous goods (hazmat) ≥ 50 lb per package** are prohibited.

Must also comply with the seller agreement, Program Policies, Restricted Products policy, and all laws.

## Dangerous goods (hazmat)
Many ordinary consumer products are regulated: **cleaners, spray paint/aerosols, cosmetics,
lithium batteries (and devices containing them), magnetized items (speakers/headphones)**, anything
flammable/pressurized/corrosive. There is **no classification by product type** — the finished
product's chemical/physical properties decide. You must provide a **safety data sheet (SDS)** /
exemption and get it classified; unconfirmed units may be disposed without reimbursement (14
business days to comply). Use "Look up an ASIN" to check classification.

- **Hazmat classes PROHIBITED from FBA:** Class 1 (explosives), 2.3 (toxic gas), 4.2 (spontaneous
  combustion), 4.3 (dangerous when wet), 6.2 (infectious), 7 (radioactive).
- **Allowed in LIMITED quantities:** 2.1 flammable gas, 2.2 non-flammable gas, 3 flammable liquids,
  4.1 flammable solids, 5.1 oxidizers, 5.2 organic peroxides, 6.1 toxic, 8 corrosive, 9 misc
  (incl. **lithium batteries** UN3480/3481/3090/3091, **magnetized material** UN2807).
- An **FBA Dangerous Goods program** (waitlist) lets approved sellers send more types.

## Expiration-dated products
Amazon treats anything with a shelf life as expiration-dated **even if no date is printed**:
consumables, **topicals (creams, soap, shampoo, cosmetics)**, **cleaning products**, bundles with a
consumable component, and anything with a period-after-opening (PAO) symbol.

- **Shelf-life rule:** at check-in, remaining shelf life must cover **full consumption time + 90 days**
  (e.g. 240 daily servings → 240 + 90 = 330 days). **Baby formula:** min 180 days.
- **Category minimums at arrival** (guide, not exhaustive): chips/snacks 90d; dried fruit / baby
  formula / cereal-granola / pet food 180d; beverages / dressings-oils / flour-spices 270d;
  peanut butter-jams / pasta / condiments 365d; candy 540d; canned food 540d; herbal/mineral
  supplements 730d; cleaning supplies 900d; personal-care & beauty 900d.
- **Labeling:** ingestibles must show an expiration or manufacturing date (lot # alone insufficient);
  best-by/sell-by = expiration; formats MM-DD-YYYY or MM-YYYY (medical devices YYYY-MM-DD); label
  the outside of any poly-bag/prep; one expiration date per ASIN per box. Expired-on-arrival =
  disposed, not returned. Cannot sell past printed expiration even with a regulatory exemption.

## Meltable products (heat-sensitive)
"Meltable" = melts ~155°F: **chocolate, gummies, select jelly- and wax-based products** (think also
crayons, lip balm, candles). Amazon's downloadable **Meltable ASINs list** defines them.
- **Sellable inbound only Sept 28 → April 20.** From **April 20 → Sept 28 (summer), Amazon will not
  accept** meltable shipments (may be marked unfulfillable/disposed for a fee). Submit removals
  before April 20.
- You can keep selling meltables **seller-fulfilled (FBM)** through summer with your own cold-pack.
- *(Sourcing rule: don't buy chocolate/candy/wax for FBA in spring to sell over summer — plan around
  the window, or do FBM.)*
