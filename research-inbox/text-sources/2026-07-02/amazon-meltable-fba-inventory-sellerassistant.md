## Cleaned content (condensed)

**Definition.** Amazon classifies as "meltable" any heat-sensitive consumable at risk of degrading at
155°F: all chocolate, gel/jelly candies and supplements, gummy vitamins, certain wax items (wax melts),
some cosmetics. FCs are not climate-controlled above ~75°F in summer; chocolate softens from ~85°F.

**Seasonal window (US FBA):**
- 🟢 Inbound allowed: **October 16 – April 14**
- 🔴 FBA restricted: **April 15 – October 15** (i.e. NOW, as of July — meltables are FBM-only)

**Consequences of non-compliance.** Meltables arriving at FBA in the restricted window are marked
unfulfillable; Amazon may dispose of them without approval and charge per-unit disposal fees; listings can
be suppressed on melted-product complaints; repeated issues trigger account performance flags. Remove
unsold meltables from FBA **before April 15** (removals take ~10–14 business days — act by early April).

**Checking.** Amazon publishes a downloadable Meltable ASIN List (updated frequently). Tools (e.g. Seller
Assistant's "Red Drop" icon) flag meltable ASINs during single or bulk research.

**Appeal.** Reclassification needs a manufacturer-letterhead letter: exact ASIN, statement the product
withstands 155°F for extended periods, the failure temperature, and a manufacturer contact. Even if
approved, complaints can re-suppress — policy is driven by customer experience, not lab results.

**Selling meltables Apr–Oct anyway (FBM):** insulated packaging (foam/thermal wrap), watertight bags,
gel packs (32–60°F) or dry ice (never touching food), next-day/2-day shipping only, avoid pre-weekend
shipments. Note: Amazon waives the Low-Inventory-Level fee for meltables during the restricted period.

**OA relevance.** Meltable check belongs in pre-buy compliance (fba-compliance-checker already covers
meltable as a gate); in July, any chocolate/gummy/wax lead is FBM-only regardless of margin.
