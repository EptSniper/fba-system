## The exact play to run today (reverse sourcing, step by step)

You'll need the free tools first: **Keepa** browser extension, **SellerAmp (SAS)** trial, and a cashback-comparison habit (TopCashback / Rakuten / BeFrugal). Then:

1. **Start on a friendly-brand listing.** Open an Amazon listing for a beginner-friendly, commonly-resellable brand — pick from this hint list (these are *discovery* signals, not permission to sell): **Jellycat, Crocs, Yeti, Stanley, Crayola, Elmer's, Monster Jam, LEGO, Hot Wheels, Pokemon, Owala, Carter's, Under Armour, New Balance, Hoka, Mrs. Meyer's, Native, Tonies.** Avoid IP-aggressive brands for now: Nike, Adidas, Jordan, Apple, Sony, Disney.

2. **Open the Offers tab in SellerAmp.** You'll see every seller on that listing.

3. **Click sellers with 1–100 reviews.** These are regular people running OA — copyable. (100–200+ reviews = advanced sellers, harder to mimic; skip for now.)

4. **Open their storefront.** That storefront is a list of ASINs they've already vetted and are profiting on. Tell it's an OA seller (not a brand/private-label): they carry *name brands* across many categories, not one own-brand line. Rough income read: #ASINs × ~$50/mo.

5. **Harvest 5–10 ASINs** that look like the friendly brands above and sit in the **$8–$60** sell band. For each, hit the SAS Google button to trace the retail source (brand site or major retailer — **never eBay/AliExpress**, counterfeit risk).

6. **Go down the rabbit hole.** Each storefront reveals new brands; each brand listing reveals new small sellers. Save every winning seller ID to a lead bank so next session starts warm, not cold.

## Flag the instant-rejects EARLY (so you don't waste the gate run)

Before a lead earns a row below, eyeball the Keepa chart and kill it on sight if you see any of these:

- **Amazon is the seller / holds the Buy Box** (now or ≥20% historically) → hard reject.
- **Rising offers** — new-offer count climbing well above its 90-day average → seller pile-in, price about to tank.
- **Price spike** — current price > 1.5× its 90-day average → it'll revert during FBA's ~2-week delay.
- **IP cliff** — offer count crashed and never recovered (e.g. 56 → 1) → brand filed IP complaints. Worse than any price drop.
- **Hard-gated brand** — list the ASIN in Seller Central *before* buying; if you can't get ungated, it's dead.
- **Too easy to find** (front-page clearance everyone sees) → margin already competed away.

## The gates every survivor must clear (at fba-deal-analyst, not here)

A lead becomes a *maybe-buy* only if it clears **all** of these — confirmed in SellerAmp + Amazon's Revenue Calculator:

- BSR ≤ 200,000 in category
- Estimated monthly sales ≥ 50 (Keepa yellow "sold" line)
- Offer count between 3 and 25
- **ROI ≥ 30%** after all fees + returns/prep allowance (≥25% OK *only* for non-returnable grocery)
- Profit ≥ $3.00/unit
- Sell price $8–$60
- Amazon does **not** hold the Buy Box

And remember landed cost **includes shipping** — a $10 item with $9.95 shipping is a **$20** cost.

## Manufacturing margin (the moat, for when you BUY)

You rarely find a deal already profitable — you *make* it profitable by stacking, so a competitor without the stack can't match you:

- **Coupon / signup codes** (Coupert)
- **Cashback** — compare via RevROI across TopCashback / Rakuten / BeFrugal (treat as cushion, **don't** bake it into COGS)
- **Discounted gift cards** (cardbear.com, Target Circle)
- **Subscribe & Save** (~5–15% on consumables)
- **Military / student / nurse / veteran** discounts; **tax-exempt reseller cert**; free-shipping thresholds

Buy from brand sites / major retailers only.

## Sizing your first buys ($500 budget)

- **5–10 units per product** until you see how it actually moves — do not over-commit on a first order.
- Formula: `variation monthly sales ÷ (price-competitive sellers + you)`, then cut **30–50%** for safety.
- Worst-case test: only buy if, at the **lowest historical Buy-Box price**, you'd break even or lose ≤ $1–2/unit.
- Across $300–$400 deployable, that's roughly **2–4 products** for your first run. Keep the rest as buffer.

## Anti-flag tactics when you do order

- Small first orders; pay with PayPal / Apple Pay / Google Pay, or message customer service to lift reseller flags.
- **List the ASIN in Seller Central BEFORE you buy** — if it's hard-gated and you can't ungate, don't buy it.
