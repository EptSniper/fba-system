## Cleaned content (condensed)

Premise: sourcing like 2022–2023 no longer works; four shifts define 2026.

1. **Work the search page, not the single listing.** A single-pack that isn't profitable often has
   multi-pack/bundle variants (3-pack, 6-pack, 100-pack) with a solid Buy Box and less competition —
   search Amazon like a customer would. The free **SellerAmp Quick View Simplified** extension puts sales
   rank, FBA seller count, and ASIN directly on the search results page (no click-through needed).
2. **Let AI find the discount path.** Example: Carter's toddler pajamas $16 → $30 on Amazon looks like a
   pass after fees, but a 20% signup coupon + discounted gift cards (e.g. CardBear) turns it into ~48% ROI.
   (Vendor pitch for Boxem's "Boxi" AI, but the underlying stacking logic is tool-agnostic.)
3. **Kill the gating headache in bulk.** Bulk ungate checkers (Boxem's connects to the seller account,
   ~5 brands/sec, suggests auto-ungate ASINs; beginners commonly clear 50–100 brands on first scan). While
   storefront stalking, dump the stalked seller's ASIN list into the checker and only chase what you're
   cleared to sell.
4. **The retail price is a lie.** Very few items are profitable at advertised retail — if they were, the
   price would tank in a week. Margin is *created*: stack coupons + gift cards + rewards; use a reseller
   certificate (instant 5–10%); ship via tax-free prep center; check FBM for light (<1 lb) items instead
   of defaulting to FBA. Each retail site has a repeatable trick set — learn it once, profit repeatedly.
