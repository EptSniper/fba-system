# Target Toy BOGO + Target Circle — Worth a Sourcing Run?

**Short answer: Yes, this is exactly the kind of trigger you should build a run around — but the BOGO and Circle are levers to MAKE deals profitable, not a reason to buy on their own. The deal still has to pass the gates ASIN-by-ASIN.**

This matches your operation's core sourcing principle verbatim: *"Source where the best SALE / cashback / coupon is TODAY (e.g. Target BOGO today) — then stack cashback + discount gift cards + coupons (manufacturing margin) for a moat nobody else has."* A Target toy BOGO + a Circle account is two stackable levers at a friendly retailer. Run it.

---

## Why this one is worth your time

1. **Toys are a strong category for your friendly brand list.** Your brain's friendly brands include several toy/kid lines that Target carries: **LEGO, Hot Wheels, Monster Jam, Crayola, Pokemon, Tonies, Jellycat, Crocs (kids), Sunny Angels**. Anchor the run on these — you already know the demand and IP profile.

2. **The math on a true BOGO is excellent.** Buy-one-get-one-free is effectively **50% off** when you buy in even quantities. Your normal target is ROI >= 30% / >= $3 profit per unit; a 50%-off cost basis clears that bar with a lot of room. That's the whole point of "manufacturing margin" — you're not finding a profitable deal, you're creating one.

3. **Target Circle stacks cleanly on top.** Circle is explicitly listed in your cost-engineering toolkit alongside discounted gift cards. Layer it:
   - **Circle offers / bonus** on the qualifying category
   - **Discounted Target gift cards** (cardbear.com / Raise — 2–10% off) to pay
   - **Cashback portal** (compare TopCashback / Rakuten via RevROI before you click through)
   - **RedCard 5%** if you have one
   Re-run the SellerAmp math *after* stacking, and keep cashback as a cushion — don't bake it into COGS.

4. **It's the right season to be loading toys.** Toys are a Q4 demand category. Today is 2026-06-29, so this is an off-season buy — fine if Keepa shows year-round movement, and a genuine opportunity if you can hold proven winners into the Q4 hump. Time FBA check-in by ~Nov 15 for anything you're staging for the holidays.

---

## How to run it (deal-first, not store-first)

1. **Confirm the BOGO mechanics first.** Is it BOGO free or BOGO 50%? Which exact toy categories/brands qualify? Even-quantity requirement? This determines your real per-unit cost.
2. **Filter to your friendly brands** that are in the promo (LEGO, Hot Wheels, Monster Jam, Crayola, Pokemon, Tonies…). Skip the avoid list — **Disney** is on it and a lot of Target toys are Disney-licensed, so watch for that.
3. **Validate every ASIN through the gates** — the promo does NOT skip this:
   - BSR <= 200k, >= 50 sales/mo (Keepa yellow "sold" line present)
   - 3–25 offers, price stable/rising, competition flat/falling
   - **Amazon NOT on/rotating the Buy Box** (>= ~20% = hard reject)
   - No price-spike vs 90-day avg; no IP "cliff"
4. **Check eligibility before buying.** List the ASIN in Seller Central first — if it's hard-gated, don't buy. Bulk-check the brands in Boxem for auto-ungate. Screen brands with IP Alert.
5. **Run SellerAmp math on the stacked landed cost** (true cost = price after BOGO + Circle + GC discount, plus tax baked into cost price, plus ~$0.50–1.00/unit prep). Use Max Cost as the reverse check.
6. **Size small.** 5–10 units per ASIN on anything new. Worst-case rule: only buy where you'd still break even at the lowest historical Buy-Box price.

---

## Watch-outs specific to this run

- **Don't buy because it's BOGO — buy because the ASIN passes.** A free second unit of something that doesn't sell is dead inventory, not profit.
- **Disney licensing.** Disney is on your avoid list; a large share of Target toys are licensed. Filter it out, especially on a young account.
- **Order-cancel / reseller flags at Target.** Start with small orders, pay with PayPal/Apple/Google Pay, and if flagged, lift it via Target live chat (this has worked before).
- **BOGO often forces even quantities** — make sure both units of each pair are sellable ASINs, not "one good toy + one dud."
- **Gift-card sourcing takes a beat.** If you're buying discounted Target GCs for the stack, line them up before the promo ends.

---

## Bottom line

Build the run. A toy BOGO at Target + a Circle account is a textbook "go where the deal is today" trigger and a clean discount stack on a category full of your friendly brands. The promo is what makes thin deals profitable — but the buy/no-buy decision is still made one ASIN at a time on Keepa + SellerAmp + eligibility. Anchor on LEGO / Hot Wheels / Monster Jam / Crayola / Pokemon / Tonies, dodge Disney, validate each lead, stack Circle + discounted GC + cashback, and test small.
