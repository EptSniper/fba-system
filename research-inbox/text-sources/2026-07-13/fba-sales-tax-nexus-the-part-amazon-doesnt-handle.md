# Amazon FBA Sales Tax Nexus Explained

Key points (distilled; full article at source URL):

## The core mental model
- Separate two adjacent-but-different files: **"Amazon collected tax on the order"** vs **"have we checked
  whether the business has any registration, filing, income-tax, franchise-tax, or documentation obligation
  in that state."** These get collapsed into one bucket constantly; the bucket is too small.
- Marketplace-facilitator rules = **one mechanism, not a full tax department.** They tell you who collected on
  a facilitated sale under a specific state's rule; they don't tell you whether your registration file is
  complete, whether a non-Amazon sale escaped the marketplace bucket, or whether a state notice is unread.

## Where nexus still touches an FBA seller
- **Economic nexus** = based on sales into a state (often $100K-style or transaction-count thresholds, varies
  by state). **Physical nexus** = physical presence; **FBA inventory stored in a state** can be part of that.
- You may know the customer state but not have treated the *inventory* state as a compliance input.
- This is a CPA/SALT specialist question — inventory location, thresholds, registration history, old sales
  channels, and entity structure all change the answer.

## Practical sequence (before registering anywhere)
1. Export Amazon marketplace tax-collection reports.
2. Pull revenue by ship-to state (Amazon AND non-Amazon).
3. Identify states where non-marketplace sales may cross economic thresholds.
4. Review whether FBA inventory creates physical-presence questions.
5. Check existing registrations + whether they created filing obligations.
6. Ask a CPA/SALT specialist before registering just because a blog made you nervous.
- Caution: **registering "everywhere to be safe" creates a filing calendar (incl. zero returns) that outlives
  the original anxiety.** The non-Amazon channel (Shopify/wholesale/Walmart/eBay/manual invoices) is where it
  gets untidy — facilitator collection doesn't reach back into those.

## Data / valuation angle
- Of 8,416 valuations with channel data: 80.1% Amazon-only or mostly-Amazon; FBA-heavy.
- Submissions **with tax returns averaged a 2.67x implied multiple vs 2.10x without** (n=5,586). Read as a
  documentation / financial-maturity signal, not a pure sales-tax finding.
- Before a sale, the useful tax file is boring evidence: where you registered, what you filed, what Amazon
  collected, what non-Amazon channels collected, who advised you, what remains open. Clean documentation makes
  uncertainty easier to price.
