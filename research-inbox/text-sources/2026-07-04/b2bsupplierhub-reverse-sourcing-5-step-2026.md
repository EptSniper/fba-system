## Distilled content

**Framing:** reverse sourcing's highest-value use is NOT finding new products — it's finding second and third authorized sources for products you already sell, so one supplier's stockout or price hike can't kill your best SKU. Forward sourcing = start from a supplier catalog and guess; reverse sourcing = start from proven demand and ask "who can supply this, under what conditions."

**Why it matters more in 2026:**
1. Tariff volatility — 62% of distributors expected costs to rise 10%+ in 2025 (NAW via CNBC); single-supplier dependency = one stockout from losing the Buy Box.
2. Thinner FBA margins force buying-side optimization: know what EVERY supplier charges for the same product.
3. Amazon's authorization scrutiny increased significantly in 2026 — chain-of-custody enforcement (brand → authorized distributor → you). A supplier who "has" the product via another middleman may not pass, even if the product is legitimate. 2026 question: "who sells this AND can prove they're authorized."

**The 5-step workflow:**
1. Identify a proven, profitable product (yours or visible on a listing). Note UPC, brand, MPN.
2. Trace the product back to the brand's distribution chain; mid-sized brands will often share their authorized-distributor list if your metrics are clean.
3. Confirm supplier AUTHORIZATION, not just availability — the invoice must pass Amazon's checks (full business name/address/phone/website, itemized, your business name matched exactly, dated within Amazon's window).
4. Compare all suppliers on cost + MOQ + stock + lead time + ship-from + payment terms — cheapest unit price isn't the best deal ($0.40 cheaper with 30-day lead + high MOQ can lose to slightly pricier 3-day-ship no-minimum).
5. Build a supplier bench: 2–3 vetted authorized suppliers per top SKU, BEFORE you need them.

**Limits (honest):** restricted distribution (closed authorized set), distributor approval criteria you don't yet meet, MOQ walls. Field notes: authorized-distributor counts vary by category — Sports & Outdoor ~3–5 per SKU, mainstream electronics ~20–25 per brand. Also: compliant invoices from real authorized distributors can still be initially rejected if Amazon can't verify the supplier — asking Amazon compliance to contact the distributor directly resolved it; and some distributors are brand-authorized but NOT authorized to sell to Amazon resellers specifically.
