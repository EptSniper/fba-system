# Keepa Read: Offer Count Climbing, Buy Box Sliding

## The setup
- Offer count: ~8 → ~22 in 90 days (roughly tripled)
- Buy Box: $35 → $28 (down ~20%)
- BSR: ~15k (decent, category-dependent)

## My read: a listing getting crowded, and you'd be a late entrant

Demand (BSR ~15k) is fine, but supply is rushing in faster than demand can absorb it. Offer count nearly tripling while the Buy Box drops 20% in the same window is the classic signature of a "shared" deal — the same source got circulated and a wave of sellers piled on. The price isn't sliding because demand is weak; it's sliding because 22 sellers are undercutting each other. $28 today is probably not the floor.

## Why it matters
1. Your ROI math is on a moving target — by the time units check in (2-4 wks) there could be 25-30 offers and a $24-25 Buy Box. Run numbers below current price.
2. Buy Box rotation gets thin with 22+ sellers — your share shrinks.
3. Race-to-the-bottom risk — don't be the last one in.

## What I'd check
- 90-day + 1-year view; build ROI on the 90-day average, shave another 5-10%.
- Offer-count slope last 2 weeks: still climbing = walk; flattened = wave may be cresting.
- BSR drops / sales estimate, not the rank number.
- Sell-through: monthly sales ÷ FBA offer count; >2-3 months to clear = pass.

## Bottom line

A saturating listing in an active price war, and you'd be arriving late. Unless your cost clears target ROI well below $28 and offer count has stopped climbing, I'd lean **pass** — or a small, fast quantity only.
