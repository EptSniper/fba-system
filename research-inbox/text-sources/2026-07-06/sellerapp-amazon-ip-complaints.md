## Content (condensed)

**Mechanics.** An IP complaint = a rights owner (or someone claiming to act for one) alleges trademark,
copyright, or patent infringement on your listing. Amazon does NOT investigate who's right — the
algorithmic response is immediate ASIN suppression. The flag appears in Account Health → Policy
Compliance → Intellectual Property Complaints (ASIN, violation type, sometimes complainant email).
Multiple unresolved complaints → lower Account Health rating, suspension, withheld disbursements,
future listings flagged.

**Who files.** Not always the brand: brand-protection agencies, former distributors, rogue competitors
using the brand name as cover, and trademark aggregators who buy IP specifically to fire takedowns.
Three repeat-offender categories: (1) zero-tolerance brands (Nike, Apple, Disney, LEGO, OtterBox, Funko,
Beats, Hasbro) with 24/7 monitoring bots — authentic inventory + invoices does NOT protect you;
(2) tightly gated niches (beauty, luxury watches, baby, supplements) whose automated systems flag listing
conflicts by default; (3) trademark/aggregator outfits. There is NO official Amazon IP-complaint list —
sellers crowdsource one (forums, r/FulfillmentByAmazon, Discord); the article recommends keeping your own
tracker: ASIN flagged, brand, violation type, retracted-or-ignored, lesson.

**Detection.** Check Account Health daily. Silent failure mode: a listing goes "inactive" and the seller
blames seasonality when it's actually sitting under an IP flag — cross-check Performance Notifications,
and investigate any ASIN whose traffic/sales suddenly stops.

**Response — valid complaint.** Remove the ASIN immediately (before Amazon suppresses); email the
complainant professionally, confirm removal, request retraction; submit a Plan of Action (what went
wrong / what you fixed / how you'll prevent it); keep all correspondence ≥180 days for audits.

**Response — invalid complaint.** Calm email with evidence (supplier invoices, authorization letters,
photos, trademark registration); if no retraction, escalate the full chain to notice-dispute@amazon.com.
Account Health callback can confirm whether a POA is acceptable and flag the account for internal review.
DMCA counter-notice exists for COPYRIGHT takedowns only and starts a 10–14 business-day legal clock in
which the rights owner may sue — use only with documented proof and ideally legal counsel.
