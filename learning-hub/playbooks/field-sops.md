# Field SOPs — the structured rules (distilled from the transcripts)

Clean, retrieval-friendly rules so the AI (and you) answer fast and consistently. This is the
"turn transcripts into rules" layer — higher signal than raw video captions.

## Good-product criteria (a buy candidate hits ALL of these)
- **BSR (sales rank) ≤ ~200,000** (sells fast enough; roughly top 1% of its category).
- **≥ ~50 sales/month** (Keepa "yellow line" of rank drops; more is better).
- **ROI ≥ 30%** after all fees (cushion for returns/price dips).
- **≥ $3 profit per unit** (below that, fees + time aren't worth it).
- **Seller band: ~3–25 offers** — at least a few (so it's a real OA listing, not private-label/wholesale), but not a crowd.
- **Price stable or rising** over 90 days; **competition stable or falling**.
- **Amazon NOT on the listing / not dominating the Buy Box.**
- **You can actually source it** profitably after tax/shipping (confirm in SellerAmp).

## Instant red flags (pass, or only test tiny)
- Amazon holds or rotates onto the Buy Box (≥ ~20% of the time).
- Offer count **rising fast** (e.g. 12 → 45) — a seller pile-in; price about to tank.
- **Price spike** vs the 90-day average — likely to revert.
- < 3 sellers or a single seller = probably private-label/wholesale (you can't just join).
- IP-risky brand (Nike, Adidas, Jordan, Yeezy, Apple, Sony, Disney…) on a new account.
- No real movement (flat rank, no drops) = it doesn't sell.
- Gated/restricted/hazmat/meltable/expiration item you can't handle (verify eligibility first).

## Keepa chart-reading rules (what each line tells you)
- **Sales rank (green)**: lower = sells faster. Each **drop** ≈ a sale. Many drops = good velocity.
- **Buy Box / New price (pink/blue)**: want it **stable or trending up**. A jagged crash = price war.
- **Offer count**: flat or falling = healthy; **rising fast = avoid**.
- **Amazon (orange) "in-stock" band**: if Amazon is frequently in stock, expect to lose the Buy Box — usually pass.
- **Buy Box rotation %**: even if a 3P holds it now, check how often Amazon/others win it.
- **Seasonality**: look for repeating yearly humps (e.g., Q4, back-to-school) — time your buy.
- **"Sold out then rank spiked"**: demand exists but supply gapped — can be an opportunity if you can restock.
- **Worst-case price**: read the lowest the price has gone; make sure you'd still break even there.
- *SellerAmp tells you if it's profitable TODAY; Keepa tells you if it's been profitable OVER TIME — use both.*

## SellerAmp settings (set these once, correctly)
- **Inbound shipping** per lb (e.g. ~$0.30–0.50/lb) — wrong here = wrong profit.
- **Minimum ROI** (e.g. 30%) and **minimum profit** ($3) so the color/verdict matches your rules.
- **Prep cost** — since Jan 2026 you prep/label US FBA yourself; bake in ~$0.50–1.00/unit.
- What it surfaces: eligibility/gating, IP alerts, private-label warnings, Amazon-on-listing,
  BSR + estimated sales, max cost (the most you can pay and still hit ROI), offer count, and profit.
- **Reverse sourcing:** from the offers section you can open a seller's storefront to find more of their products.

## Discount-stacking SOP (you MAKE products profitable by lowering buy cost)
Beginners wrongly expect profit at the shelf price. Stack every lever:
- **Cashback portals** (Rakuten, TopCashback) — click through *before* you buy.
- **Discounted gift cards** (cardbear/Raise) — instant 2–10% off.
- **Coupon / email-signup codes**; **retailer rewards**; **subscribe & save**.
- **Free-shipping thresholds**; **tax exemption** (resale certificate); **bulk/quantity** breaks; **holiday sale codes**.
- Re-run the math *after* stacking — many "bad" deals become buys.

## Storefront-stalking SOP (best beginner method)
1. Start from one good ASIN or a known arbitrage seller's storefront.
2. List the **3P sellers** on the listing; pick the ones that look like real OA sellers (not Amazon, not the brand).
3. Open their storefront → pull their **brands + ASINs**.
4. Filter junk (Amazon-dominated, gated, low rank, crowded).
5. Run survivors through the **Deal checks** above (SellerAmp + Keepa).
6. Save the good ones as leads. (Counterpoint to weigh: some sellers call this mediocre — combine it with brand-mining, don't rely on it alone.)

## Brand-mining / Keepa Product Finder SOP (great for seasonal)
1. Pick a known-good brand (Crayola, Puma, Cuddle Duds, Gap…).
2. In Keepa Product Finder: filter **BSR ≤ 150k–200k**, **remove Amazon in-stock**, require **~3–5 offers**.
3. Sort by sales-rank drops (velocity) or recent price increase.
4. Find a retail source for the survivors; run the deal math.
*(Back-to-school / Q4: add the seasonal brands and check 90-day price movement.)*

## Q4 & seasonal rules
- Get FBA shipments in by **~Nov 15** so they're checked in for the holiday rush (Q4 check-in can take 1–3 weeks).
- Don't over-buy random holiday-themed junk; **double down on proven winners** — normal products also spike.
- Consider **FBM** in Q4 when FBA check-in is too slow (one case study did $72k in December all FBM).

## Buy-quantity & risk
- **Test small first** (5–10 units) on anything new; scale only after it sells through.
- Buy more only when: strong rank, stable/rising price, falling competition, source price holds after tax.
- Never bet the bank on one ASIN; spread risk across leads.

## Ungating quick rules
- Sell what you're **already approved for** first; expand later.
- **Auto-ungate** check (e.g. Boxem) before sourcing a brand.
- **Invoice ungating:** real invoice from an authorized distributor — typically 10+ units across multiple invoices, real business name/address, dated recently. **Never fake invoices.**
- Avoid hard-gated/IP brands on a young account.
