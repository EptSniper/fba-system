# Nike Air Force 1s from a Nike Outlet — Should You Flip Them on Amazon?

**Short answer: No, not as your plan stands.** The "double your money" math is real, but on Nike sneakers from a retail outlet it almost never survives Amazon's gating, IP, and authenticity rules.

## 1. The eligibility problem (the dealbreaker)

**Nike is a gated brand for most sellers.** The majority of accounts cannot list new Nike without brand approval ("ungating"), and Nike approvals have been largely closed for years. Scan the exact ASIN in the Seller app: if you see "You need approval to list this brand," you're gated.

**Even if listed, authenticity is the next wall.** To sell Nike you generally need a valid invoice from an authorized Nike distributor. An outlet receipt does NOT qualify. An "inauthentic" complaint → Amazon asks for a supplier invoice; a till receipt fails → listing pulled, funds held, account can be suspended — even though the shoes are genuine.

> Eligibility verdict: For a typical seller, Nike sneakers from a retail outlet are BLOCKED — by brand gating AND the invoice/authenticity standard.

## 2. The profit math is worse than "double"

On a ~$110 AF1 bought at ~$55: referral ~15% -$16.50, FBA (bulky) ~$8–12, inbound+prep ~$3–5 → ~$22–28 net (40–50% ROI) only if it sells at full price. Shoes have high return rates and brutal price competition. Downside (suspension) dwarfs the ~$25/pair upside.

## 3. Eligibility FIRST, profit SECOND

1. Scan the ASIN in the Seller app in-store. Gated → walk away.
2. Only if ungated for Nike AND shoes: can you produce an authorized-distributor invoice? Outlet → no → walk away.
3. Pull the Keepa chart per ASIN/size.
4. Only then run real deal math with a returns buffer.

## Verdict
- Eligibility: BLOCKED for a typical seller.
- Risk: High — Nike IP/inauthentic complaints can suspend your account.
- Profit: Overstated.

**Recommendation: Don't grab them.** Confirm gating in Seller Central; if not ungated (you almost certainly aren't), pass.

*Operational guidance, not a guarantee — only your live Seller Central can confirm gating. Never auto-buy.*
