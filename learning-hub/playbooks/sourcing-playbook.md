# Sourcing Playbook (Online Arbitrage)

*The complete, step-by-step product-finding system distilled from the SellerAmp
tutorials, the Keepa-Finder session, and 3 live sourcing marathons. This is the
"how I actually find products" reference — and the spec for the scout AI.*

---

## SellerAmp settings (copy these first)
Set once in SellerAmp → Settings so the profit math is accurate:

- **Inbound shipping:** ~$0.60/lb (range $0.50–0.80; re-check after each real FBA shipment and adjust).
- **Min BSR 0% / Max BSR 2%** (top 2% of the category).
- **Min profit:** $3/unit (under $3 usually isn't worth the time).
- **Min ROI:** 30% (covers Amazon's lenient returns). ~25% OK for non-returnable grocery.
- **Prep fee:** enter your prep-center per-unit fee if you use one.
- **Sales tax:** bake it into the **cost price** (total ÷ units), not the % field — don't double-count.
- Plan: start **Getting Started** (1,000 lookups/mo, ~$20); upgrade to **Getting Serious** (unlimited, ~$28) when you hit the cap. 14-day free trial.

## Two ways to source

### A) Reverse sourcing / "storefront stalking" (best for beginners)
The #1 method. Other sellers already did the research — copy them.

1. On any name-brand Amazon listing, open the **SellerAmp Offers** tab → see every seller.
2. Click into sellers with **1–100 reviews** (regular people like you; 100–200+ = advanced, harder to copy).
3. Their **storefront** shows every product they sell → these are vetted OA leads.
4. Analyze each (see "Read the chart" + "Run the math"); the good ones, find the retail source via the **Google button**.
5. Go down "rabbit holes" — each storefront reveals new brands; each brand reveals new sellers. Repeat for hours.
   - Tell that a seller is OA: they carry name brands (Nike, Jellycat, Yeti, Crocs…) not private label.
   - Estimate their income: **# ASINs × ~$50/mo** (conservative).

### B) Keepa Product Finder (scalable, when you know a brand)
1. **keepa.com → Data → Product Finder.**
2. **Brand** = your target (e.g. Crayola → ~8,600 listings).
3. **Sales Rank: 0 → 150,000–200,000** (top ~1%; drops to ~1,000).
4. **Amazon: out of stock** (don't compete with Amazon; removes ~300).
5. **New offer count: min 4–5** (ensures real OA competition, not single-seller/wholesale) → ~430 left.
6. *(Optional)* Sort by **90-day drop %** to surface items priced well above their 90-day average (spike windows).
7. Open the survivors and analyze.

### C) Manual sourcing (only on sites you know well)
Browsing a retailer's clearance/sale pages in real time. Slow and not recommended for beginners,
but catches live deals. Favor mainstream colors (black/white/gray); skip odd colors.

## Read the Keepa chart (the core skill)
Want to see, on 3-month + 1-year views:

- **Buy Box price (pink):** stable or trending up → resale price is trustworthy.
- **Offer count:** flat or **declining = good** (sellers selling through). **Rising = avoid** — price will tank.
- **Oscillation vs blocky:** lots of up/down wiggle in Buy Box + offers = **selling fast**; long flat "blocky" stretches = **slow**.
- **Yellow "sold" line:** present (e.g. "50 sold", "100 sold") = sells **≥50/mo**. **No yellow line ⇒ <50/mo ⇒ usually pass.**
- **BSR:** lower = faster; check 1-year for **seasonality**.
- **Buy Box rotation:** spread across many FBA sellers = good. **One seller or Amazon holding ~80–100% = skip.**
- **Historical offers:** sellers on the listing for months = it's been profitable.

### Instant-reject red flags
Rising offer count · IP cliff (sellers crash, e.g. 56→1) · Amazon owns ~100% Buy Box · no Buy Box/featured offer · hard-gated brand.

## Run the SellerAmp math
For each candidate, confirm:
- **Eligible / ungated** (the panel tells you).
- **ROI ≥ 30%** after all fees (≥25% OK for non-returnable grocery), **≥$3 profit/unit**.
- **True landed cost** — include shipping! A $10 item with $9.95 shipping = **$20** cost; enter that.
- Use **Max Cost** as a reverse calculator (the max you can pay and still hit your ROI).
- No-Buy-Box listing? You must match the **lowest price** to sell (slower).
- Packaging slightly different but functionally identical → usually fine (think like the customer).

### Cost engineering (turns thin deals profitable)
Stack any of: signup/coupon codes (**Coupert**), **cashback** (compare via **RevROI**: TopCashback, Rakuten, BeFrugal), **discounted gift cards** (**gift card wiki**, Target Circle), **Subscribe & Save** (~5–15%, cancel before next ship), rewards programs, tax-exempt, bulk programs.
Math: 15% off → cost × 0.85. **Don't bake cashback into COGS** — use it as a cushion.
Buy from **brand sites / major retailers only — never eBay or AliExpress** (fake risk).

> **"Manufacturing margin"** (Miles): you rarely find a deal already profitable — you *make* it profitable by
> stacking discounts. Add **cardbear.com** (discounted gift cards, with price-drop alerts) and
> **military / student / nurse / first-responder / veteran** discounts (Home Depot etc.), plus free-shipping
> thresholds (5+ units). A **tax-exempt / reseller certificate** removes sales tax at Home Depot / Lowe's / Walmart.

### Pricing & Keepa tips (Masterclass)
- **FBA pricing gap:** if the Buy Box is an FBM seller and there's a gap up to the lowest FBA price, price your FBA
  offer **5–10% above the Buy Box** — you don't have to be the cheapest.
- **Keepa graph settings:** hide sub-ranks; Graph Settings → show **offer counts + ratings**; deselect rating/FBM;
  read the **3-month** view. Reject pattern = **offer count UP + price DOWN**. Check **all-time** for an IP
  **"cliff"** (sellers crash to ~1) and **avoid brand-generic** listings (not under a real brand).
- **Bulk ungate check:** paste ASINs into **Boxem's** bulk ungate checker while storefront-stalking to instantly see
  what's auto-ungated (see [ungating-playbook.md](ungating-playbook.md)).

### Deal-first sourcing + extra red flags (sourcing course)
- **Start where the deal is TODAY.** Don't grind a store with no sale — go where there's a **sale / high cashback /
  BOGO** right now (Walgreens BOGO today vs Target nothing → Walgreens), then stack discounts. The whole
  [deal-sourcing system](../ai-system/deal-sourcing-system.md) automates this.
- **Storefront-stalk low-rating sellers** (sub-10 / no ratings = your level, easy finds, often auto-ungated). Save
  winning **seller IDs** to a lead bank. Judge by the **buy-box price**, not a seller's inflated list price.
- **More instant rejects:** **price spike** (current price ≫ its 3–12-month history → it reverts, brutal with FBA's
  ~2-week delay); **seller-count spike** (inverse to price → race to the bottom); **too-easy-to-find** (if you found
  it in 5 min at Walmart/Nike, so will everyone — prefer low-eye sites like Grocery Outlet); **brand on the listing**
  (the brand owner is a seller → they kick resellers off); **Keepa "cliff"** (seller count crashes and never recovers
  = IP complaints).
- **Screen IP risk with IP Alert** (Chrome extension that flags brands known for filing IP complaints — trust it over
  SellerAmp's IP flag unless that one is RED).
- **List before you buy.** Try to list the ASIN in Seller Central first; if it's **hard-gated**, don't buy it.
- **Beat order-cancels** (retailers flag resellers/fraud): start with **small orders**, pay with **PayPal / Apple /
  Google Pay**, or **message customer service** to lift the flag (works well — e.g. a Target reseller flag removed via live chat).

## Size the buy
- **Beginner:** 5–10 units/product until you know how it moves.
- **Advanced formula:** `units/month you can capture = variation monthly sales ÷ (price-competitive sellers + you)`, then cut **30–50%** to be safe.
  - Per-variation sales: open **Variations → sort by ratings**, take that variation's **review % × total estimated sales**.
- **Worst-case rule:** only buy where, even at the lowest historical Buy-Box price, you'd **break even or lose ≤$1–2/unit**.

## Philosophy (from the case studies)
- **"Base hits, not home runs."** Most products make $100–200/mo; a few make $1,000+. Build a **wide catalog, go deep on winners.**
- **"Fast nickel beats a slow dime."** Prefer faster sellers so cash keeps turning; realized ROI beats "potential" ROI.
- **Break even first** as a new account to build sales velocity → Amazon trust → easier ungating.
- **Reps.** First products take 10+ hours to find. Most people just never put in the time.
- **Spend-$X/day** to hit a revenue goal; scale via **more inventory + VAs + prep center**, not more hours.
