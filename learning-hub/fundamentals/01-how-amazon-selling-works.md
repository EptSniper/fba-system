# 01 · How Selling on Amazon Actually Works

*Written for a complete beginner. Goal: understand the whole machine before any money moves.*

---

## The one-sentence version

You list a product on Amazon's marketplace, a customer buys it, Amazon (or you)
ships it, Amazon takes a cut of the sale, and the rest lands in your bank account.

Everything else is detail on those five steps.

## You are a "third-party seller"

Amazon sells some things itself ("1P" — first party). But most of what you see is
sold by independent sellers like you on the **Amazon Marketplace** ("3P" — third
party). You get a **Seller Central** account (the seller dashboard at
`sell.amazon.com`) and that's your control panel for listings, orders, payments,
and reports.

There are two account plans:

| Plan | Cost | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Individual** | $0.99 per item sold, no monthly fee | Testing with very low volume (<~40 sales/mo). Can't win the Buy Box, can't apply to most restricted categories. |
| **Professional** | ~$40/month ($39.99), no per-item fee | Anyone serious. Needed for the Buy Box, restricted-category approval, bulk tools, and reports. |

Once you're past ~40 items/month, Professional is also *cheaper*. (More in
[03-fees-explained-simply](03-fees-explained-simply.md).)

## Listings, ASINs, and the Buy Box (the part beginners miss)

- Every product on Amazon has one **listing** identified by an **ASIN** (Amazon's
  10-character product ID, e.g. `B0XXXXXXXX`).
- Crucially, **many sellers can sell on the same listing**. If you sell a product
  that already exists on Amazon (which is exactly what arbitrage is), you don't
  make a new page — you add your offer to the existing ASIN.
- When multiple sellers share a listing, Amazon picks **one** offer to feature in
  the "Buy Now" / "Add to Cart" box. That's the **Buy Box** (now called the
  "Featured Offer"). **~80%+ of sales go through the Buy Box.** Winning it depends
  on price, being on FBA, seller health, and stock. Individual-plan sellers can't
  win it — another reason to go Professional.

> This is the key mental model: **selling an existing product = competing for the
> Buy Box on a shared listing.** Creating your own product (private label) = owning
> your own listing with no Buy Box competition.

## FBA vs FBM — who does the shipping

| | **FBA** (Fulfilled by Amazon) | **FBM** (Fulfilled by Merchant) |
|---|---|---|
| Who stores & ships | Amazon, from its warehouses | You, from home |
| Prime badge | Yes (big conversion boost) | Only via Seller-Fulfilled Prime |
| Customer service & returns | Amazon handles it | You handle it |
| Cost | Per-unit fulfillment + storage fees | Your own time + postage |
| Buy Box advantage | Strong | Weaker |

Most arbitrage and private-label sellers use **FBA**: you send inventory to
Amazon, and they pick, pack, ship, and do customer service. You trade fees for
scale, the Prime badge, and a stronger Buy Box position.

## What happens in a single sale (the flow)

1. You source a product and create/join its listing in Seller Central.
2. (FBA) You ship the units to an Amazon warehouse; they're now "in stock."
3. A customer buys — ideally because you have the Buy Box.
4. Amazon picks, packs, and ships it with Prime.
5. Amazon deducts its fees (referral + FBA fulfillment + any storage).
6. Every ~2 weeks, Amazon **disburses** your balance to your bank account.

## Where the money goes (rough shape)

For a typical item, Amazon's cut (referral + FBA fees) is often **20–40% of the
sale price**. Add your **cost of goods** and shipping, and what's left is profit.
That's why beginners must do **fee math before buying** — the single most
expensive mistake is discovering the fees ate the margin *after* paying for stock.
(Tools like SellerAmp and Amazon's Revenue Calculator exist exactly for this.)

## The honest baseline

- You must be **18+** to hold a seller account.
- Nothing here guarantees income. **Online/retail arbitrage** can start small
  ($500–$2,000 of inventory). **Private label** realistically runs $2,500–$5,000
  and 3–6 months to a first profitable month.
- The skill that makes money is **disciplined product/deal evaluation**, repeated.
  The tools and this hub make that discipline faster — they don't replace it.
