# Amazon Featured Offer Overhaul: Rank-Only Buy Box Rules (Digital Applied)

Directly relevant to OA because winning the Featured Offer (Buy Box) is the whole "can it profit" endgame
for a listing you don't control.

## What Amazon announced (Jul 6, 2026)
- Via the official `News_Amazon` Seller Forums account: **seller performance is no longer a standalone
  pass/fail eligibility requirement for the Featured Offer.** Rationale: "the first seller eligibility step is
  no longer delivering additional value to customers, so we're removing it."
- Amazon says it is **"not changing how the Featured Offer is selected"** — still evaluates on competitive
  pricing, delivery speed, and performance — and warns "being considered doesn't guarantee your offer will be
  featured." **No seller action required**; offers are auto-included as the rollout reaches each marketplace.

## The mechanic: gate-then-rank → rank-only
- **Old:** two steps — (1) pass a standalone seller-performance eligibility check, then (2) get ranked among
  the passers on price/delivery/service.
- **New:** one step — the signals that powered the gate (**chargeback rate, Order Defect Rate, Voice of the
  Customer complaints**) become **direct weighted inputs inside a single ranking score**, alongside price,
  free shipping, and delivery promise. Account health now scores *continuously* instead of gating once.
- Key nuance (per analyst Daniel Rijo, Havas Media): a weak account isn't "freed to win" — its defects still
  count, blended continuously, so it **competes and loses** rather than being excluded. **Structure changed,
  criteria didn't.**

## Timeline (phased, one confirmed leg)
- **EU + UK: reported effective Jul 20, 2026** (ChannelX) — the first firm date anywhere.
- **Global: gradual across all stores, completing by end of 2026** (Amazon via ppc.land). No country-by-
  country schedule; **US lands on an unannounced date before year-end** → do the re-audit now.
- Compounding July change for FBM sellers: tightened **Seller Fulfilled Prime delivery thresholds took effect
  Jul 6, 2026**; losing the Prime badge typically drops an offer below the Featured Offer threshold.

## Weights are undisclosed
- Nobody outside Amazon can model how heavily chargebacks/VOC count vs price/speed. **Treat any specific
  weighting % in vendor decks / LinkedIn takes as invented.**

## The ad-spend knock-on (the part nobody covers)
- Per Amazon Ads docs: **without the Featured Offer, Sponsored Products / Brands / Display serve ZERO
  impressions** — "regardless of campaign status." A campaign can read **Delivering** and generate 0
  impressions.
- Eligibility is **SKU-specific**: for multi-SKU ASINs, only the SKU holding the Featured Offer serves ads;
  siblings get 0. Holding Featured-Offer-eligible listings is a hard account-level prerequisite for running
  Sponsored Products at all. → Monitor **impression volume per advertised SKU**, not campaign status.

## Factors that did NOT change
- Customer reviews / star ratings are still **not** a selection factor. Prime/FBA-vs-FBM still excluded as a
  tie-breaker (but fulfillment still shapes the speed/service inputs). Professional-account requirement is a
  separate, unaffected gate. Featured Offer is still determined **per variation** and can **rotate** among
  best-ranked offers by shipping location / Prime / filters.

## Re-audit before rollout reaches you
Repricing rules + off-Amazon price-bot exposure (an open, unanswered question); move ODR/chargeback/VOC from
threshold alerts to weekly **trend** dashboards; harden Sponsored campaign structure against SKU-level
Featured-Offer flips; baseline current Buy Box share per marketplace so post-rollout drift is measurable.

## Our-project read
No ai-brain threshold change. But this reinforces that a healthy account (low ODR, low chargebacks) now
affects Buy Box *rank continuously* — relevant to the "can it profit" axis and to any future account-health
signals in the control-center. Buy Box share will get noisier during each marketplace's transition window.
