# Amazon Seller Accounting and Bookkeeping (2026 Guide)

Core thesis: in 2026, with rising FBA fees, storage charges, ad costs, and tax complexity,
Amazon seller accounting/bookkeeping is a competitive necessity, not optional. High sales ≠ high
profit; hidden costs quietly eat margins without a structured system.

## Accounting vs bookkeeping
- **Bookkeeping** = daily recording of transactions (sales, returns/refunds, Amazon fees, ad spend,
  inventory purchases).
- **Accounting** = interpreting financial health (P&L, balance sheet, cash-flow, tax planning,
  forecasting). Answers "are you really making money?"
- You need both.

## Setup (6 steps)
1. **Separate business money** — dedicated business bank account + card; mixing personal funds corrupts
   the books.
2. **Pick accounting software** — QuickBooks / Xero + an Amazon integration tool; manual spreadsheets
   fail at scale.
3. **Link Seller Central** — sync orders, refunds, FBA fees, ad costs.
4. **Reconcile Amazon settlement reports** — Amazon pays ~every 2 weeks; match gross sales, subtract fees,
   adjust refunds, verify deposits. Reconciliation is the single most important step; "Sales" and "Deposits"
   are different numbers.
5. **Track inventory & COGS correctly** — FIFO for COGS, landed-cost calculation, storage-fee allocation.
   Inventory mistakes destroy margin accuracy.
6. **Monthly financial reports** — P&L, cash-flow, inventory valuation.

## Common mistakes → fixes
- Not reconciling settlement reports → reconcile every 2 weeks.
- Ignoring refunds/chargebacks → always adjust for them (they lower real revenue).
- Miscategorizing Amazon fees → separate referral / FBA / storage / PPC categories.
- Poor landed-cost tracking → can't compute true profit without it.
- Mixing personal + business costs → tax overpayment + audit risk.

## Taxes & compliance (2026)
- Amazon collects marketplace-facilitator sales tax in many states, but the seller still owes income tax.
- Cash basis simpler (fine for most sellers under ~$1M revenue); accrual gives more accurate period
  profitability and is required over ~$1M gross receipts.
- Clean books lower audit risk and overpayment.

## Reports to review monthly
P&L, cash-flow statement, inventory valuation, expense breakdown — for margin, liquidity, efficiency.
