# Amazon FBA Fees, Mid-2026: The Real Cost Math (Digital Applied)

Myth-busting piece separating three distinct 2026 fee events that seller blogs keep merging into
one imaginary "July overhaul." Every figure is recomputed from Amazon's live rate pages.

## The one thing that actually changed on July 1, 2026
- Amazon **ended prep and item-labelling services for FBA shipments in the Canada store** on Jul 1, 2026
  ‚Äî the sequel to the US ending the same services on **Jan 1, 2026** (announced Aug 2025). Applies to
  direct-to-FBA, Amazon SEND, and Supply Chain Portal inventory.
- **Grace period:** shipments *created* before Jul 1 still get prep even if they arrive later. Shipments
  created after Jul 1 without proper prep still ship, but **lose reimbursement eligibility** if items are
  damaged or become untraceable. Deadline was the shipment-*creation* date, not arrival.
- Read the Canada move as a forward signal for UK/EU/JP.

## The "180-day storage overhaul" is NOT July news (mythbust)
- A content-farm cluster claims a "revised storage schedule took effect Jul 1, 2026," "$0.87/cu ft"
  standard storage, and "aged-inventory now triggers at 180 days instead of 271." **None survives a check
  against Amazon's live pages.**
- Standard-size storage is **$0.78/cu ft (Jan‚ÄìSep)** and **$2.40/cu ft (Oct‚ÄìDec)** ‚Äî NOT $0.87. Budgeting
  $0.87 overstates the storage line ~12% before the Q4 multiplier.
- The **181-day aged-inventory entry tier is the pre-existing baseline** (Amazon's own "before Jan 16, 2026"
  table already shows it). The 271‚Üí181 shift predates 2026; it is current, not new July news.

## The real H2 driver: January's fee overhaul (eff. Jan 15‚Äì16, 2026)
- No new fee *types* (one narrow exception: packaging fee on non-SIPP Bulky). Amazon's stated average net
  effect: **~$0.08/unit, under 0.5% of avg selling price** (a vendor estimate averaging winners and losers).
- Small standard-size avg **+$0.25/unit**; large standard-size ~+$0.05; items >$50 saw Small +$0.51 / Large
  +$0.31; sub-$10 Small +$0.12 (low-price discount widened ~$0.86/unit).
- **New consolidated $0.60/unit inbound defect fee** replaces the prior double-charge on shipments that
  don't arrive / arrive late/abandoned / land at the wrong location.
- **Aged-inventory:** 366+ day minimum doubled $0.15 ‚Üí **$0.30/unit**; new **456+ day tier** at $7.90/cu ft
  or $0.35/unit whichever is greater.
- Not uniformly upward: removal/disposal fees for small aged items (<0.5 lb) fell ~$0.20; Bulky split into
  Small/Large Bulky (lower default fulfillment ~‚àí$2.06 / ‚àí$0.26); Extra-Large fell ~$2.08/unit. Low-inventory
  fee now assessed at **FNSKU level** (each variation on its own depth); grocery exempt.

## April's 3.5% fuel & logistics surcharge (eff. Apr 17, 2026)
- 3.5% surcharge on **all** US + Canada FBA fulfillment fees (+ Remote Fulfillment to CA/MX/BR); extended to
  Buy with Prime + MCF on May 2. It's a **percentage of the (already-raised January) fee**, so apply it
  LAST when modeling H2 landed cost or you understate it.

## H2 playbook
