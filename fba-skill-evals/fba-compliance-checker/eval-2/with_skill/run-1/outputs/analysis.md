# Eligibility Analysis — Crayola Markers

**Question asked:** "I heard Crayola is easy to get ungated. Am I allowed to sell them?"

This answer covers **only the "Am I allowed?" half** of a buy decision — eligibility, account risk, and restrictions. It does **not** tell you whether the deal makes money. That profit question (Keepa demand, ROI, fees, Buy Box) is a separate analysis handled by the deal analyst, and a "yes" here never implies a "yes" there.

---

## The honest limitation, stated up front

Eligibility is **account-specific**. It is ultimately decided in **Seller Central / SP-API**, not by me or by any brand list. I can flag risk and likelihood, but I cannot truly clear an ASIN for *your* account. The decisive, non-negotiable test is **list-before-you-buy**: try to create the offer in Seller Central first. If it is hard-gated for your account, don't buy it — no matter how cheap or how "friendly" the brand is.

"Crayola is easy to get ungated" is a real and common observation, but two cautions:
1. It is a **hint to look closer, not permission to sell**. Friendly-brand status is a discovery signal, not eligibility proof.
2. "Easy to ungate" still means there may be an **approval step** (a category/brand gate you have to clear) before you can list — easy is not the same as already-open.

---

## ELIGIBILITY CHECK — Crayola (markers)

- **Gating:** **Approvable / often auto-ungated — must verify in Seller Central.** Crayola is on the operation's *friendly* list (commonly resellable / commonly auto-ungated). Many accounts can either list immediately or clear the gate easily. But markers fall in arts-and-crafts / school supplies, and brand- or category-level approval can still apply on your specific account. Treat as **approvable, confirm by listing**.

- **IP / brand risk:** **Low.** Crayola is explicitly a friendly brand in the operation's brand hints, not on the avoid-list (which is Nike, Adidas, Jordan, Yeezy, Apple, Sony, Disney). No standing signal that Crayola aggressively kicks resellers off listings. *Still verify per-ASIN*: check the live listing for an IP Alert, confirm the brand owner is not selling on its own listing, and scan the Keepa offer-count history for an "IP cliff" (offer count crashing toward ~1 and never recovering) — that fingerprint can appear on any single ASIN regardless of the brand's general reputation.

- **Hazmat / meltable / expiry / oversize:** **None apparent for standard washable/classic markers.** Standard Crayola markers are water-based and not typically hazmat, not meltable, not dated grocery, and are small/light (standard-size). Caveat: do not assume this for every Crayola SKU — **scented markers, paint markers, certain art markers, and aerosol/spray products can carry solvent/flammability (hazmat) flags**. Check the specific ASIN.

- **Condition / variation notes:** Sell **new** only. Crayola markers are frequently sold as **multipacks and variation listings** (color counts, pack sizes, washable vs. classic vs. Super Tips, etc.). On a variation (parent/child) listing you can be **eligible on some child ASINs and not others**, and the pack/count you physically buy must match the exact child ASIN you list against. Verify at the child-ASIN level, not the parent.

---

## VERDICT: ALLOWED (verify)

Crayola markers are a **low-IP-risk, commonly-resellable, friendly brand** with **no inherent hazmat/meltable/expiry blockers** for standard washable/classic markers. There is **no brand-level reason to block**. The only thing standing between you and listing is the **account-specific gate check**, which for Crayola is usually easy to clear.

This is "ALLOWED, pending verification" — not a green light to buy. It clears the eligibility axis only.

---

## Decisive next step

1. **List the exact ASIN in Seller Central before buying.** If you can create the offer, you are ungated for it on your account. If it shows "Approval required," follow the ungating path (it is typically a quick, often automatic approval for Crayola). If it is hard-gated, do not buy.
2. **Confirm the specific SKU is a standard water-based marker** (not a scented/paint/aerosol variant that could be hazmat).
3. **Verify at the child-ASIN level** so the pack/count you buy matches the listing, and check the live listing for an IP Alert and the Keepa offer history for an IP cliff.
4. **Hand the profit question to the deal analyst** — this analysis does not say the deal makes money.
