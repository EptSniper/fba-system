# Compliance check: scented candles + hand sanitizer (clearance, Q4 FBA)

**Question:** "Thinking about sourcing a bunch of scented candles and some hand sanitizer for Q4, both on clearance. Am I good to send these into FBA?"

**Short answer:** Not yet. Both categories carry FBA eligibility/restriction risk that is separate from whether they'd be profitable. Verify each one in Seller Central before you buy. The fact they're on clearance adds two more red flags (condition/expiration and Q4 timing). Below is the verdict per item.

This is an eligibility / account-risk review only. It does NOT tell you whether either flips for a profit — run the deal math (ROI, fees, BSR/sell-through) separately. And nothing here grants real eligibility; only your Seller Central account can confirm what you're allowed to list.

---

## 1. Scented candles — VERIFY (lean cautious)

Candles are the classic FBA "meltable / hazmat" trap. Two separate problems:

- **Meltable / heat-sensitive.** Wax candles soften and deform in heat. Amazon treats meltables specially: in many fulfillment centers meltables are only accepted **Oct 1 – Apr 30** (the cool months) and are restricted or removed in summer. Since you're targeting Q4, the calendar is actually in your favor here — but you must confirm the ASIN is accepted, not assume it.
- **Hazmat / flammable review.** Scented candles can be flagged hazmat because of flammability and the fragrance oils. A hazmat-flagged ASIN goes through a dangerous-goods review and may need an SDS (safety data sheet) before FBA will accept it. Some candle ASINs are FBM-only as a result.

**Brand/IP angle:** if these are a brand that polices its listings (e.g., Yankee Candle and similar are well-known arbitrage targets but also actively brand-gated on some accounts), check that you're ungated on the exact brand before committing. Our brand "avoid" list (Nike, Adidas, Jordan, Yeezy, Apple, Sony, Disney) doesn't include candle brands, so IP is likely lower-risk than meltable/hazmat here — but confirm gating per brand.

**Before buying, confirm in Seller Central / SellerAmp:**
1. The exact ASIN shows **"This product can be listed in New condition"** for your account (not gated, not FBM-only).
2. It is **not flagged hazmat/dangerous-goods** (SellerAmp surfaces this; Seller Central "manage inventory" shows a hazmat status), or if it is, that you can supply the SDS.
3. It's accepted into FBA **right now** as a meltable (Q4/winter window helps, but verify the ASIN isn't blocked).

## 2. Hand sanitizer — VERIFY (lean cautious / often blocked)

Hand sanitizer is higher-risk than candles for a new/young account:

- **Topical drug / OTC + restricted.** Hand sanitizer is regulated as an OTC drug (it makes a kill-germs claim). "Health & Personal Care" / topical-OTC items are frequently **gated** and often require approval, and some sanitizers are outright restricted on FBA.
- **Flammable / hazmat.** Alcohol-based sanitizer (typically 60–70%+ ethanol/isopropyl) is **flammable = hazmat/dangerous goods.** Expect a dangerous-goods review and possible SDS requirement; gel and liquid forms have shipping limits.
- **Expiration-dated.** Sanitizer carries an expiration date. FBA enforces expiration rules (and meltable-style date labeling); short-dated stock can be refused or removed. This is exactly why **clearance** stock is risky — clearance often means it's close to expiry.

**Before buying, confirm in Seller Central / SellerAmp:**
1. Whether the ASIN/brand is **gated** for your account (topical OTC commonly is) and whether you can get approval.
2. **Hazmat status** and whether you can provide an SDS.
3. **Remaining shelf life** — FBA typically wants a healthy expiration buffer; verify the clearance lots aren't short-dated.

## 3. The "clearance" angle (applies to both)

Clearance is great for buy cost, but it changes the compliance picture:

- **Condition/expiration:** clearance consumables (sanitizer especially) are often near expiry. Short-dated stock can be rejected at check-in or removed. Photograph/verify expiration dates before shipping.
- **Buy as New, sell as New:** make sure clearance units are sealed, undamaged, and genuinely New condition — clearance shelves often have dented/opened units that can't go in as New.
- **Replenishability:** clearance is a one-time buy. Fine for a flip, but you can't restock if it sells well.

## 4. Q4 timing note (eligibility-adjacent, not strictly compliance)

- Get FBA shipments checked in by **~Nov 15** — Q4 check-in can take 1–3 weeks.
- Meltable candles: the Q4/winter window is when meltables are *most* likely accepted, so timing works in your favor — but it also means inventory sitting past Apr 30 may get restricted. Sell through in-season.
- If FBA check-in is too slow in peak Q4, **FBM** is a fallback for items FBA won't take fast enough.

## Verdict

| Item | Eligibility verdict | Main blockers to clear |
|------|--------------------|------------------------|
| Scented candles | **VERIFY** (cautious) | Meltable window, possible hazmat/SDS, per-brand gating |
| Hand sanitizer | **VERIFY** (often BLOCKED) | OTC/topical gating, hazmat/flammable + SDS, expiration on clearance stock |

**Do not send blind.** For each ASIN, before you buy:
1. Check **"Apply to sell" / listing eligibility** for your account (gated? FBM-only?).
2. Check **hazmat/dangerous-goods** status (SellerAmp eligibility panel + Seller Central). Be ready to supply an SDS if flagged.
3. For sanitizer, confirm **expiration buffer** on the clearance lots and whether OTC approval is required.
4. Confirm the **meltable acceptance window** for the candle ASINs.

If any of those comes back gated/hazmat-blocked/short-dated and you can't clear it, that unit is BLOCKED for FBA regardless of how good the clearance price is. Of the two, candles in Q4 are the more workable; hand sanitizer is the one most likely to stop you cold.
