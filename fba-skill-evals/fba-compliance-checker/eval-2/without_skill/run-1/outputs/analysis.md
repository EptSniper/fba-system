# Crayola Markers — "Am I allowed to sell them?"

**Verdict: VERIFY (likely ALLOWED after ungating)**

Crayola is not a blocked or hard-gated brand for you. It's on the project's known-good, OA-friendly brand list. But "easy to get ungated" is not the same as "already allowed." You almost certainly need to get approved (ungated) first before you can list, and there are a couple of product-specific things to confirm.

---

## 1. Brand / IP risk — GREEN

- **Crayola is on our OA-friendly brand list** (a repeatedly-cited beginner winner, alongside Elmer's, LEGO, Hot Wheels, etc.).
- It is **not on the AVOID / hard-gated list** (Nike, Adidas, Jordan, Yeezy, Apple, Sony, Disney — brands that file aggressive IP complaints or only let grandfathered sellers in).
- No history of aggressive IP-complaint behavior is flagged for Crayola.

You are not at risk of being blocked outright or hit with an IP complaint just for reselling genuine Crayola. Good brand choice for a beginner.

## 2. Gating / ungating — THIS IS THE REAL GATE

You heard right that Crayola is "easy to ungate," but **easy != no approval needed.**

- Crayola is **gated** — most accounts must **Apply to sell -> Request approval** before they can list it. It's generally cited as a **~200-unit ungate** request, BUT in practice **~10 units on a valid invoice is usually accepted.**
- It's beginner-friendly to ungate because the invoice is cheap to source:
  - **Best source: target.com** — Target receipts say **"Invoice"** (not "Order Confirmation"), which is what Amazon requires.
  - Crayola via Target runs roughly **$0.50–$8/unit**; ~10 units cited at ~$80 (or far less for budget items).
- **Always click "Apply to sell -> Request approval" first** — sometimes Crayola instantly auto-approves (a hidden auto-ungate). You won't know until you try.

### How to ungate (quick version)
1. Seller Central -> search the **specific Crayola ASIN** -> **New** condition -> **Apply to sell -> Request approval**.
2. If documents are asked for, **buy ~10 units of a Crayola product from Target** (or the brand site) and get the **invoice PDF**.
3. Invoice must say **"Invoice,"** show **your Seller Central shipping address**, and list **item name + quantity**.
4. Upload the PDF. Strengthen it by combining order-confirmation email + invoice + photo of the 10 units laid out + delivery email into one PDF, plus a short cover letter.
5. If **denied in under ~5–10 minutes**, a bot auto-denied it — don't just resubmit. Wait 24–48h, try a **different Crayola ASIN**, or reply to the case. You want it to sit "under review."

> "Ungate for free" by returning the 10 units within Target's window exists as a tactic — use sparingly and within Target's policy.

## 3. Product-specific checks — VERIFY before you buy

- **Markers specifically:** standard Crayola markers are not hazmat, meltable, or expiring, so they're typically straightforward for FBA. Still **run a quick FBA-eligibility check on the exact ASIN** — some art/craft items (certain paints, solvents, aerosols, specialty lines) can trip **hazmat** flags. Plain washable/classic markers should be fine.
- **Toys & Games seasonality:** Crayola often sits in Toys & Games. In **Q4 (~Oct–Dec), Amazon imposes seasonal sell-through / performance requirements for new sellers in Toys.** It's June now, so not immediate, but if sourcing for Q4 confirm you meet the holiday selling-eligibility threshold first.
- **Variation traps:** Crayola listings have many variations (pack size, color, washable vs. classic). Be approved for and list the **exact** variation/ASIN you buy.
- **Condition:** sell **New** only as a beginner.

## 4. Bottom line

- **Allowed?** Not instantly — Crayola is gated, so **get approved (ungated) first.** Once ungated, you're clear to sell genuine new Crayola: low IP risk, on our friendly list.
- **Easy?** Yes — one of the cheapest, most beginner-friendly brands to ungate (Target invoice, ~10 units, low cost).
- **Before buying inventory:** (1) apply to sell + complete ungating on the specific ASIN, (2) confirm the exact marker ASIN passes FBA hazmat/eligibility, (3) if sourcing for Q4, confirm Toys seasonal selling eligibility.
