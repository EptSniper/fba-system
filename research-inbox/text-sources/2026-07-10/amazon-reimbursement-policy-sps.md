# Key Amazon Reimbursement Policy Details, Changes, and Updates

Vendor-adjacent (SPS Commerce sells reimbursement software) but well-sourced summary of how
Amazon's FBA reimbursement policy has changed 2024–2026. Captured because the reimbursement
regime materially affects OA unit economics and the "returns/loss buffer" assumption in ai-brain.

## Timeline of critical changes [policy]
- **Aug 23, 2024** — MCF reimbursement caps introduced (max compensation by country).
- **Oct 23, 2024** — US claim window tightened to a 60-day deadline for most claims.
- **Nov 1, 2024** — US automation launch: automatic processing for some claim types.
- **Jan 9, 2025** — UK claim window tightened to 60 days.
- **Jan 15, 2025** — UK automation launch.
- **Mar 31, 2025** — **Cost-based (sourcing-cost) reimbursement model** takes effect.

## Sourcing-based (manufacturing-cost) reimbursement [policy]
- Amazon now reimburses lost/damaged FBA inventory on the item's **manufacturing / sourcing cost**,
  NOT the selling price. Took effect 2025; reduced payouts for many sellers.
- Sellers can review/submit their own cost data via the **Inventory Defect and Reimbursement (IDR)
  portal** (Seller Central G66ZLS453YSE2Y4R). If you don't supply cost data, Amazon estimates it.
- Reimbursement generally excludes shipping, labeling, packaging, and other operational costs.

## Claim windows (post-change) [policy]
- Fulfillment-center ops claims (warehouse-damaged, lost in transit): **file within 60 days**.
- FBA customer-return claims (US): **file between 60–120 days** (UK 45–105).
- Removal claims (lost in transit): file 15–75 days; other removal claims within 60 days of delivery.

## Automatic vs. manual claims [policy]
- **Automatic:** lost inventory, damaged inventory, customer-return issues.
- **Manual (seller must file):** removal claims, mishandled returns, other/misc errors.

## MCF reimbursement caps (Aug 2024, unchanged as of 2026) [policy]
- AU $450 AUD · CA $400 CAD · FR/DE/IT/ES €275 · MX $5000 MXN · UK £250 GBP.
- Caps may not cover high-value items → consider third-party insurance for high-ASP inventory.

## Practitioner takeaways [practitioner]
- Keep COGS / landed cost current per SKU so cost-based reimbursements aren't lowballed.
- With most windows now 60 days, audit reimbursement/returns reports on a tight cadence.
- Returnless resolutions: refunded items are NOT returned to inventory (no resale/inspection);
  seller absorbs both refund and product cost — factor into thin-margin, high-return categories.
