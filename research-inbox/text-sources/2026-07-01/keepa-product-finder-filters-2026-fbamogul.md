## Distilled takeaways

- **[practitioner]** Keepa Product Finder has ~120 filters across 800+ input fields. **Filter logic:** between different sections filters use AND (a product must satisfy every section you set); within a section, ranges apply. Don't over-constrain — each added section shrinks the result set multiplicatively.
- **[practitioner]** Use the **Sales Rank (BSR)** filter on every search — it's the single best indicator that a product actually sells. Prefer the **90-day average BSR over current BSR**, because current rank swings intraday. Most sellers set a max BSR to avoid slow movers.
- **[practitioner]** Beware the "Out of stock" checkbox meaning — it is **not** "product unavailable." On **Buy Box Price** it means *no Buy Box winner* (all offers too expensive, poor seller metrics, or Amazon suppressed the BB). On the **Amazon price** filter it means *Amazon's own offer is absent*. These are different signals; read the label per filter.
- **[practitioner]** A core OA/wholesale play: filter for **Amazon out of stock**, which finds listings where Amazon stopped selling and left the Buy Box open to 3P sellers. But "Amazon OOS right now" can be a temporary stockout — pair it with the **90-day OOS %** and **Amazon OOS Count** filters (Refine section) to confirm it's a persistent pattern, not a blip.
- **[practitioner]** Set a **minimum price** (~$8–£10+, category-dependent) to screen out items that can't clear FBA fees even if bought for free; set a **maximum price** to your sourcing budget. Price **drop %** fields surface falling prices (clearance opportunities) or rising prices (growing demand).
