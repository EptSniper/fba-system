## Cleaned content (condensed)

1. **Brand bulk sourcing** — list favorite brands in Excel separated by three hashtags (`###`) and paste
   the list into the Product Finder brand filter to pull all brands at once (Nick Spisak workflow).
2. **Price stability filter** — Buy Box "90 days drop %" set to a tight band like −20…20 finds
   stable-priced products; the closer to 0, the steadier the pricing (avoids yo-yo listings).
3. **Negative keywords** — prefix a minus (e.g. `-cooktop`) in keyword fields to exclude irrelevant
   variants when hunting a niche (e.g. Whirlpool replacement parts).
4. **Storefront stalking via PF** — search a competitor's favorite ASINs/brands in Product Finder to
   surface similar items and reveal which brands they favor.
5. Watch a filter walkthrough live (small filters like review count give niche results).
6. **Rankless gems** — deliberately skip the sales-rank filter: items with no rank but steady price
   history + growing reviews are ignored by rank-filtering competitors yet can carry real demand.
7. **"Corridor" method (Chris Grant)** — pick a tight price band (e.g. $15–25) + strict demand filters
   (reviews, rank); re-run the same corridor weekly for a steady stream of qualifying products.
8. **Wholesale leads** — filter for consistent Buy Box owners where Amazon + a handful of sellers share
   the BB; steady-demand categories (personal care, kitchen) → vendor-relationship targets.
9. **Bookmark queries** — Keepa PF URLs encode the exact filters (API-backed); URL-decode saved bookmarks
   to learn how advanced queries are constructed, and save your own for reuse.
10. **Amazon-to-Amazon flips** — filter for recent low Buy Box prices, check for a dip/spike cycle, buy
    dips and sell peaks within Amazon (thin margins, volume-driven).
11. **Ten refresh searches** — low seller count, seasonal keywords, small/light items (fee savings),
    high review counts, niche categories, loyalty brands, low-ranked ASINs, price-sensitive/repriced
    items, accessories for popular devices, and price-fluctuation windows.
