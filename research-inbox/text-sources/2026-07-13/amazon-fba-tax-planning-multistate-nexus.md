# Amazon FBA Tax Planning 2026: Multi-State Nexus

Key points (distilled; full article at source URL):

## Nexus from FBA inventory
- The moment Amazon places ONE unit in a fulfillment center in a state, you have **physical sales-tax
  nexus** there. No minimum threshold. You don't pick the warehouse — Amazon's algorithm distributes
  inventory across 20+ states by demand forecast.
- Nexus is typically assessed on a rolling-quarters basis, **by state, not federally** — no federal nexus
  standard exists. CA, NY, IL are aggressive pursuers of FBA sellers.
- Retroactive risk: states can look backward and assess prior periods. **Register before inventory arrives.**
- Action: run the **Inventory Event Detail report** in Seller Central to see where stock is stored.

## What marketplace-facilitator laws cover — and don't
- As of 2026, all **45** US sales-tax states have marketplace-facilitator laws → Amazon collects+remits on
  facilitated marketplace sales. Five states have no general sales tax: AK, DE, MT, NH, OR.
- Amazon does NOT handle: (1) **non-marketplace sales** (Shopify/own site/wholesale — you collect yourself in
  nexus states); (2) **local/city taxes** (AZ, CA, CO may need separate filings); (3) **income tax**;
  (4) **property tax on inventory** (some states tax business personal property incl. warehoused inventory);
  (5) **zero-dollar returns** still required in many states or you get late-filing penalties (10–30%).

## Multi-state income tax
- Physical presence (inventory) can trigger **income-tax nexus**, not just sales tax. Income apportioned by
  sales factor (10% of sales into CA → CA may tax 10% of business income).
- CA most aggressive: **$800 annual LLC fee** for any FBA seller "doing business" (incl. inventory) there.
- Voluntary disclosure programs offer reduced penalties + limited look-back (3–4 yrs) vs unlimited.

## Deductions most sellers miss
- Highest-value fix: **break out Amazon settlement reports** — referral, FBA fulfillment, storage, ads,
  returns as separate lines, not one "sales" entry. A $200K-revenue seller typically has $50–70K deductible
  beyond COGS; missing $10K in deductions ≈ $2,500 wasted at 25% effective rate.
- Checklist: Amazon fees, PPC/DSP, COGS (freight/duties/inspection), software (JS/H10/A2X/Taxomate), product
  dev (photography/samples/prep/labels), home office, vehicle mileage ($0.67/mi), professional services,
  education, financial costs, insurance.

## Entity structure
- **LLC → S-Corp election at ~$50K–$100K net profit** saves most FBA sellers ~$9K/yr in self-employment tax
  (pay a reasonable salary — 40–60% of profit — take rest as distribution, $0 SE tax on distribution).
- **File Form 2553 by March 15** for retroactive election to Jan 1. C-Corp almost never right for FBA
  (double taxation, effective rate can exceed 40%).

## Year-end checklist
Inventory Event Detail report → map nexus across all channels → review entity (Form 2553 by Mar 15) →
Section 179 / bonus depreciation → reconcile settlements monthly → quarterly estimated payments →
property tax on inventory (assessed often Jan 1 — timing is a legit lever) → consider voluntary disclosure.
