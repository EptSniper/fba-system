## Content (condensed)

Context: Amazon eliminated its own FBA prep and item-labeling services in the US store effective
Jan 1, 2026 (per Snapl's linked companion article). From 2026, sellers assume full responsibility for
labeling, packaging, and prep accuracy BEFORE inventory reaches a fulfillment center — FCs no longer
correct mistakes at check-in. Upstream errors (factory, consolidation, 3PL) now mean receiving delays,
rejected inventory, unplanned service fees, or stranded units.

**Unit-level labeling.** One clear scannable barcode per sellable unit (FNSKU or manufacturer barcode
depending on listing configuration). Most common failure: multiple competing barcodes on one unit →
mis-scans/misidentification. Labels on flat accessible surfaces, no curves/seams, no glare/wrinkles/tape
over the code. Polybagged/overwrapped units: barcode must scan through or sit outside the bag — "exists
but can't be scanned quickly" counts as non-compliant. Expiration-dated products: date clearly visible,
consistently formatted, not hidden by packaging layers.

**Packaging.** Polybags with large openings need suffocation warnings (top compliance failure, easiest to
prevent); weak seals, distorted barcodes, too-thin bags are the other polybag failures. Sets/bundles/
multipacks must arrive as ONE physical unit with a single barcode for the whole bundle — loose components
almost always mis-receive. Liquids: secure closures, tamper evidence, secondary containment when risky,
moisture-protected labels. Fragile: cushioning that prevents movement; "fragile" stickers don't compensate
for weak packaging.

**Carton level.** Each carton must contain exactly what the shipment plan says + one unique, unobstructed
box label. Common errors: labels on seams/box tape, covered by stretch wrap or glossy tape, multiple labels
too close, smearing prints. Palletized cartons: labels must stay readable after wrapping.

**Practical checklist.** Unit: 1 scannable barcode, flat placement, sealed polybags w/ warnings, bundles
contained + labeled as one. Carton: correct quantities, one readable label, not on seams/under wrap.
Process: scan-test samples, visual QC on first runs, standardized packaging spec per SKU.
